# Getting Started — expanded 13-week session map (with FC1 teen-led second-runnings)

**Status:** draft for John's review, dev-only. Companion to [`fc1-companion-track-SPEC.md`](fc1-companion-track-SPEC.md). Once locked, this expands **Section 1 (Session Map)** and **Section 9 (Session-by-Session Sketch)** of the Word handbook.

## What changed from the 10-week map, and why

The pilot showed most practice processes never get the whole group through in one session. So three practice-heavy weeks each gain a **second running** — same process, same scripture, the rest of the group takes their turn — and on that second running **a senior (Companion-in-Formation) leads, protocol in hand,** with an adult present. That's the "see one → do one" engine *and* the peer-engagement engine, in the live cohort.

**+3 sessions, all second-runnings:** Telling Your Story II (Wk 4), PROAPT II (Wk 8), Garden II (Wk 10). Nothing new is taught in them — they *complete* a process the cohort already began. The four-Connect sequence and its dependencies (Self → Others → God → Mission) are preserved.

## How leadership is distributed

- **The container repeat-runs every week for free.** From Wk 5 on, a senior takes the open or the close each week — by then they've watched it 4–5 times. This is the steady, low-risk rep that builds container fluency (FC1's spine).
- **The three named second-runnings** (Wk 4, 8, 10) are the marquee teen-led slots the whole cohort watches.
- **Use a team of Companions-in-Formation — as many willing seniors as clear the gate (no cap).** Each senior's parent + the Lead Companion form that senior's discernment pair. Spread the runnings so several peers lead and no single teen is overloaded — the cohort seeing more than one of their own step up is the point.

## What stays adult-held, always

Confession & restoration (Wk 6) and Any Doubts? (Wk 11) elicit disclosure directly — their **centers are never teen-led**, even on a second running. A senior may still open/close the container for those sessions. And in every 🟡 slot the safety line holds: **the young leader runs the process; an adult holds whatever surfaces.** In the dorm, where no adult is in the room, that "whatever surfaces" is caught by the referral reflex and the covering relationship (SPEC §3 #4–5).

## The Leader Feedback Round (standing practice after each teen-led slot)

After each teen-led running (Wk 4, 8, 10, and any container running), run the **Leader Feedback Round** — affirmation first, growth second, group feedback only by the leader's consent, popcorn-style so only those with something to say speak. Full protocol in [SPEC §5b](fc1-companion-track-SPEC.md). This is the loop that turns seeing-it into confident-leading.
