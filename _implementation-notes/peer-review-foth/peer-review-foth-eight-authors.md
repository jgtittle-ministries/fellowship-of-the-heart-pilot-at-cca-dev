# Fellowship of the Heart (FotH) Pilot — Cross-Author Synthesis

> **What this is.** A simulated, charitable-strengthening peer review of the deployed FotH cohort curriculum by eight voices (four academic — Keller, Stott, Fee, Willard; four companion — Foster, Eldredge, Prince, Lewis), read through three primary lenses: **pastoral safety, child safeguarding, and IJH fidelity.** Every reviewer was asked to *commend warmly, then press hard.* This is a **stress-test, not an endorsement** — the affirmations are real, but the document's purpose is to surface what to strengthen before more minors walk the pilot. Every consensus concern was then **adversarially verified** against the actual files (line-checked, grep-checked, refutation paths walked); concerns that the verifier downgraded or refuted are marked as such, and the punch-list is weighted by **what survived verification**, not by how many reviewers raised it.

## Headline verdict (all eight)

All eight reviewers **commend the pilot warmly and send it back with substantial revisions required before more minors enter** — none recommends against it, and several call it the most pastorally mature draft formation curriculum they have reviewed. The corpus's deepest strength is named by every voice: *"You will not be the agent of transformation… create the conditions, then get out of the way"* — VIM rightly ordered, the Spirit (not the apparatus) as the agent of change, applied consistently from the Letter to the Companion through the Garden week's *Note for the Skeptic*, the diagnostic-not-therapeutic boundary, the non-coercive discernment guide, and the per-week crisis scaffolding. The presses cluster where a confident architecture forgets its own warnings: a **labeled crisis "single source of truth" that is one cross-reference short of the actual life-safety steps**; **no safety line on any page a distressed teen actually holds alone**; **trauma-adjacent depth and the forgiveness-vs-safety question handed to 12–14-year-olds gated only by simpler words**; **grace mechanized into a circuit/percentage in the confession and knot work**; the **cross assumed rather than preached**; and the **larger Story / the believer's authority stripped out of the very places a young heart most needs them.** Fix the safety chain and the placeholders first; then re-voice the mechanism language and put the gospel and the Story back at the center — and this becomes, in the panel's shared judgment, the strongest formation on-ramp for the young any of them has reviewed.

# Part 1 — Cross-author synthesis

## (a) Editor's overview

This is a rare review to write, because the curriculum is good enough that the panel's hardest presses are *acts of friendship toward a strong draft*, not triage of a weak one. The FotH safety architecture is, by the independent scan's own words, "unusually mature and remarkably consistent for a draft" — all 34 weekly plans carry a CRISIS CONTINGENCIES block; the deep-work weeks add WATCH FOR blocks with youth-specific reporting and self-harm steps; consent, the two-adult rule, parallel-circle disclosure protection, competency tiers, and a gnostic-closure guard are present and applied across all three series. Eight reviewers converged on the same short list of real defects, and adversarial verification then did its job: it confirmed the two most important findings, **sharpened** several, and **refuted or downgraded** a handful that turned out to be already-handled in text the reviewers had not fully traced.

The honest shape of the result: the **child-safeguarding and pastoral-safety findings largely survived** (the SSOT chain, the participant-facing safety line, the forgiveness-vs-safety guard, junior depth-gating, the placeholder launch-gate, the confession redirect, the household reconciliation pressure). Several **theological/de-mechanize findings were downgraded** once the verifier found the gospel warrant or the affect-guard already sitting a few lines down (most notably the confession-week "mechanizes grace" concern, **refuted**, and the John 8:44 eisegesis concern, **refuted**). The Garden "un-vetted word for a week" concern was **refuted** — the same-evening correction is already in the framing script and the take-home handout. Weight the punch-list accordingly: **do the safety chain and the child-safeguarding line-edits regardless; treat the de-mechanize items as polish where verification softened them.**

## (b) The one change that matters most

**Make the named crisis "single source of truth" actually contain the steps — and put a participant-facing safety line where a distressed teen will see it.** These are two halves of one priority: the responder-facing chain and the participant-facing surface.

Going Out Section 8 and Appendix C (and the week-file "Default" pointers) name **`going-deeper/handbook.md` Section 6** as "the single source of truth for crisis contingencies." But GD Section 6 (line 374) only *references* Getting Started and adds amplifications — the actual seven-step suicidal-ideation/self-harm sequence and five-step abuse-disclosure sequence (with the teen-specific *"call the parent before the teen leaves the building / call CPS first if the parent's response would endanger the teen"* line and 988/911/CPS) live one hop further back, in **`getting-started/handbook.md` Section 6 + Appendix C.** Verification confirmed the chain is real and is a broken-promise cross-reference for the most safety-critical content — while **right-sizing it to medium** (the pocket Crisis Quick-Reference Card is the true first-reach instrument and already carries the condensed steps; the steps are not absent, the *label* is one hop short). The fix: **inline the full step sequences into GD Section 6** so the named SSOT is terminal, *or* re-label every pointer as "Getting Started Section 6 + Appendix C, carried forward."

The participant-facing half **survived at full HIGH severity**: the entire crisis architecture is Companion-facing, and the Personal Heart Journal — the private, take-home artifact a 12–18-year-old writes their hardest material into, alone, at night, with the deep-work weeks deliberately surfacing heavy material into it — carries **no** "if you are in danger, here is who to tell / 988" line. Add a warm, non-alarming standing footer to the journal (right after the "No one but you" privacy promise, which is exactly what makes a teen feel they have nowhere to turn), the shared cards, `start-here.md`, and the Family Conversation Cards. **Keep CPS/mandatory-reporting language off participant artifacts** (correctly facilitator-only); point to people and 988.

## (c) Consensus affirmations — the pilot's spine

These are the load-bearing strengths the panel asks you to *protect* while you revise:

1. **"You will not be the agent of transformation… create the conditions, then get out of the way"** (`getting-started/handbook.md`, Letter to the Companion), placed first — named by **all eight** as the corpus's deepest strength and surest de-mechanize guard.
2. **The Garden of Your Heart week** (`getting-started/week-07-garden.md`), especially **Handout H7.3 "A Note for the Skeptic"** — the most-praised single session (seven reviewers): honors the Reformed conscience as "a form of faithfulness," anchors imaginative prayer in Ps 23 / John 15, holds what surfaces *under* Scripture, refuses to generate revelation, preserves a real opt-out ("silence is a complete answer").
3. **The clinical-referral boundary** ("not therapy… process-oriented, not result-oriented… the team is NOT credentialed for that work in this room") — stated cleanly in `going-deeper/handbook.md` Section 5 and re-applied at every deep-work week.
4. **The pre-cohort discernment guide** (`getting-started/precohort-discernment-guide.md`) — a model of non-coercive invitation; honors "NO — and honest," refuses to over-celebrate "yes," lets a teen's no override a parent's yes, refuses to promise outcomes.
5. **The "integrated vs. closed / Companion-as-Pharisee" analysis** (`going-deeper/handbook.md` Section 5) — the most gospel-aware self-correction in the pilot, tied to supervision ("every Companion has a Companion").
6. **The corporate-listening guards** (`week-07`/`week-08`) — 1 Cor 14:29 weighing treated as load-bearing, "I-heard/I-sensed" enforced over "God told me you should," gnostic-closure named and policed (Fee: best-guarded lay corporate-hearing work he has seen).
7. **The child-safeguarding scaffolding** — two-adult rule, background checks, Virginia mandatory-reporting with statute + CPS number, the "do not name methods" redirect, "call CPS first if the parent's response will endanger the teen," per-week CRISIS CONTINGENCIES + WATCH FOR, parent-as-participant / parallel-circle protection, depth gated out of the mixed-age setting.
8. **The theology of returning** — `shared/rhythm-card.md` and the Personal Heart Journal: "Streaks are not the goal. Returning is the goal… The Spirit meets honest journals; He cannot do much with performances of journals." Reviewers urge quoting it *into* the handbooks.
