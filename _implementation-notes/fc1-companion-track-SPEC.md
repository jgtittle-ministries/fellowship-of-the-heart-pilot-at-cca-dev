## 0. What changed in the brief

Original ask: teach teens to *co-lead blocks* inside an adult-run cohort.

Clarified ask (this is bigger and better):

1. **FC1 is the first level of Formation Companion formation — and a legitimately *stable* landing place.** We are not building "apprentices in transit to adult Companion." We are forming teens *to FC1*, which for many will be where they stably and honorably serve for years, maybe for life.
2. **The real telos is autonomous facilitation.** A senior teen who, a few years later, can implement *all of Getting Started* on their own — the college-dorm scenario.
3. **Peer-leadership is the engagement engine.** Teens watching a peer open the container will pull the cohort forward "like no other process."

This spec answers: what *is* FC1 for this context; what makes someone dorm-ready; whether we need more sessions; and how the in-cohort apprenticeship is built.

## 1. FC1 as a real, possibly-terminal level

Frame FC1 the way Vol 5 frames the Formation Companion progression and the way the Leader Levels doc already frames its ladder: **integration, not replacement.** FC1 is not "not-yet-FC2." FC1 held with integrity — a person who can build and hold a container, walk the leadable practices, know their limits, and refer well — is a *complete* competence. Someone who stays at FC1 their whole life and runs a faithful dorm group or living-room group has arrived, not stalled.

**FC1 competence, concretely (this is the target we form to).** Pilot-grounded and deliberately modest — John watched debut seniors reach exactly this: *significant confidence leading the opening/closing container and simple scripted processes (Garden of Your Heart, PROAPT), nothing deeper.* That floor **is** FC1:

- Can **establish and hold the four container conditions** (Safe / Present / Clear / Intentional) from memory, under mild pressure, without reading a script.
- Can **run the leadable band** of Getting Started — the container, the teaching frames, the process practices (PROAPT, the Four Connects teaching, diagnostic setups) — cleanly.
- **Knows the boundary** of FC1: what they can hold and what they must route to someone above their pay grade — instantly, without heroics.
- Is **doing their own four-Connects work** and can name where they are in it. (Handbook rule: "you cannot lead someone where you have not been.")
- Can **speak a specific, witnessed blessing** over a person — the one relational skill that most carries the container.

FC1 explicitly does **not** include: care leadership over disclosure (crisis, confession depth, wound work), which is FC2+ and, in this pilot, adult-and-clinically-backed only.

## 2. The telos: the college-dorm FC1 (why it's a *different* safety problem)

The dorm scenario clears one hard problem and opens another.

**Clears:** at ~19–21 in a dorm, the leader is an **adult leading adult peers.** The entire "minor leading minors" legal blocker (the reason the CCA version needs Virginia legal review + CCA written sign-off) **does not apply.** No parental-consent apparatus, no minor-over-minor question.

**Opens:** the dorm has **none of the CCA safety net.** At CCA the net is institutional — two adults in the room, a named clinical/pastoral backup on call, a mandatory-report chain, a head of school. In a dorm that net is *gone.* The leader is FC1 — entry level — and they are the front line with no one behind them.

So the governing readiness question for the dorm is not "can they run the exercises" (they can — that's the leadable band). It is:

> **Has this young leader internalized the boundary and built their own referral net — so that the thing the two adults and the clinician used to do, they now do reflexively, alone?**

That is the competency that *replaces* the adults in the room. Everything else is downstream of it.

## 3. The FC1 dorm-readiness profile (the specific answer)

What actually gets a senior teen ready to run Getting Started solo in a dorm in a few years. Eight competencies; the safety ones are load-bearing.

| # | Competency | What "ready" looks like | Where it forms |
|---|------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Own formation is real & ongoing** | Has genuinely walked the four Connects — told their story, sat in their Garden, named their doubts — and is still in a rhythm. Not technique over an empty interior. | GS + GD + GO as a *participant* first |
| 2 | **Container fluency from the bones** | Opens & closes the container from memory, under pressure, no script. Can re-establish it when the room's temperature climbs. | In-cohort reps (§5) + the Leader Lab |
| 3 | **The whole arc internalized + reps** | Has *led every leadable block* multiple times, and understands *why* the sequence is Self→Others→God→Mission — so it's in the body, not just in hand. | Leader Lab + repeated cycles |
| 4 | **Scope humility + referral reflex** ⚠️ | Knows exactly what FC1 cannot hold; routes crisis/disclosure instantly. Carries a *pre-built* referral map for wherever they are (campus counseling, 988, a pastor, an RA, parents they can still call). | Leader Lab — the safety core |
| 5 | **A standing covering relationship** ⚠️ | Before they start, they've *set up* an ongoing "who do I call when I'm in over my head" relationship — youth pastor, mentor, a parent with the posture. The dorm analogue of "an adult in the room." | Named at Sending; sustained across the arc |
| 6 | **Adaptation judgment** | Understands the design intent well enough to adapt GS to a peer-only context (drop the parent/parallel-circle architecture; keep the Connect sequence and the container) without breaking the load-bearing pieces. | Leader Lab; this is the higher-order skill |
| 7 | **The invite / gather skill** | Can make the ask — gather 4–8 peers, frame the covenant, set the container expectation. In a dorm no one hands you a cohort. | Overlaps the Inviting Others content |
| 8 | **Knowing what to hold back** | Holds the "What we hold back" discipline — no deep wound work, deliverance, TPM, extended intensity — in a setting with no supervision. | Leader Lab + handbook §4 |

⚠️ = the two competencies that substitute for the missing institutional net. If these two aren't solid, the leader is not dorm-ready no matter how well they run the exercises.

**Honest timeline:** this is a **1–2+ year formation across the whole arc plus real reps**, not a 10-week output. Getting Started's job is to *start* FC1 and *light the fire*; the arc + the Leader Lab + practice complete it. Your own phrase — "in a few years" — is exactly right.

## 4. Do we need more sessions? — A parallel Leader Lab, run alongside (DECIDED)

**Decision (John):** the FC1 Leader Lab runs **alongside the 10 weeks**, and we **aim to fully form FC1 within Getting Started** — a senior is dorm-capable at series end.

The participant series stays 10 weeks forming *participants*. Alongside it runs **Track B — the FC1 Leader Lab**, a parallel, opt-in, senior-only, discernment-gated track. This is where "more sessions" lives: ~6 Lab sessions for the selected seniors, not 6 more weeks for everybody.

**What "fully form FC1 in the debut cycle" means — pilot-grounded (John's correction).** This applies to **first-time seniors in their debut cycle** — John saw it happen already. It works *because debut FC1 is deliberately modest* (confident container + simple scripted processes, nothing deeper) **and because of the repeat-running mechanism the pilot revealed:**

> Most processes never get all the way through the whole group in one session — storytelling, PROAPT pairs, the Garden, even the container with a full circle take **more than one running** to include everyone. **The first running is adult-led — the teen sees it. On the second running, a teen leads it, protocol in hand.** That is "see one, do one" happening *inside the live cohort,* not in a side rehearsal — and it is simultaneously the engagement engine, because the cohort watches a peer take the second pass.

- **Opening/closing** is received every week, so a senior has seen it 3–5× before taking a running (Wk 4–6).
- **Simple scripted processes** (GotH walk-through, PROAPT) are received, then led on their *second running* — the scriptedness is why a debut senior picks them up fast, exactly as the pilot showed.

**This is why John suggested a longer series.** Repeat-runnings need room: the more sessions, the more second-running slots a teen can lead. So series length is genuinely back on the table (§10) — the leading happens *in-cohort via repeat-runnings*, which means the Leader Lab shrinks to what the cohort flow doesn't cover (safety, referral, scope, adaptation, debrief) rather than being the main place teens practice.

So **no pre-series intensive and no returning-senior assumption are needed.** The debut senior reaches **FC1 = confident container + simple processes,** which is a real, stable landing place (§1).

**Keep FC1-debut distinct from the dorm horizon.** Debut-cycle FC1 is the *floor,* formed in one GS cycle. Running *all* of Getting Started solo in a dorm is the *trajectory* that floor opens onto — built over "a few years" across the arc + real reps (the full readiness profile, §3). FC1 is fully formed in GS; dorm-full-series capability is not, and we don't promise it in ten weeks. That's how John's two choices (alongside + fully-form-in-GS) hold together honestly: the *modest* FC1 genuinely completes in the debut cycle.

**Proposed Leader Lab shape (~6 sessions, runs alongside):**

1. **Lab 1 — The container is the skill.** The four conditions from the inside; why the container (not the content) is FC1's spine. They lead a mock open/close.
2. **Lab 2 — The leadable band & the bright line.** Walk the 🟢/🟡/🔴 map (§6). Drill: "something heavy just surfaced while you were leading — what do you do?" (Answer: hand it to the adult / route it. Every time.)
3. **Lab 3 — Leading a process block.** They lead PROAPT and the Four Connects teaching end-to-end, then debrief.
4. **Lab 4 — Scope, referral, and covering.** Build *their own* referral map. Name *their own* covering relationship. This is the dorm-safety session.
5. **Lab 5 — Adapting it to your world.** How GS flexes to a peer-only dorm/living-room context without breaking the Connect sequence. The invite/gather ask.
6. **Lab 6 — Mock full session + commissioning.** They run a full leadable session for the lab; commissioned as FC1 at the cohort's Week 10 Sending.

That's the answer to "more sessions": **~6 Leader-Lab sessions for the selected seniors, not 6 more weeks for everybody.**

## 5. The in-cohort apprentice thread (the engagement engine, made visible)

Your instinct — seeing peers lead engages the cohort like nothing else — is the design's motivational core, so we make it *visible*, not a back-room rehearsal. A discernment-selected senior teen leads, in front of the cohort, with an adult beside them.

Graduated arc across the 10 weeks (see-one → do-one → teach-one):

- **Weeks 1–3 — Watch & assist.** Shadow: chairs, phone box, read the scripture, give the one-word prompt. Get the protocol *in hand.*
- **Weeks 4–6 — Co-lead the container + one process block.** By now they've seen the open run 3–4×. They co-lead it (adult beside them) and co-lead the PROAPT walk-through (Wk 6) — the cleanest teen-leadable block in the series.
- **Weeks 7–10 — Lead more + feedback.** Carry the container mostly themselves (adult present); after each leading, run the **Leader Feedback Round (§5b)** — plus a private self-reflection: *what did you notice in yourself while leading? where did you want to fix instead of hold space?*
- **Week 9 (Mission) is the theological home.** Name it here: for some of you, part of the "good works prepared beforehand" (Eph 2:10) is leading others into this. Leading isn't a job we assign — it's a Connect-4 discovery.
- **Week 10 (Sending)** commissions willing teens as FC1 — the feeder into Going Deeper → Going Out → Inviting Others, and eventually the dorm.

## 5b. The Leader Feedback Round — after every teen-led slot (John's pilot practice)

After a Companion-in-Formation leads, they get a short, structured feedback round. **Order matters: affirmation first, growth second, the group only by the leader's consent.** This is the loop that builds FC1 confidence fast — reps plus immediate, safe feedback — and it extends the curriculum's own no-fixing / no-judging ethos (it pairs naturally with the closing "blessing").

1. **Team — keep doing.** The Companion team names what the teen did well and would want to see again next time.
2. **Team — one thing to try.** The team names what they'd do differently next time — held to one or two things, not a list.
3. **Ask the leader.** "Would you like feedback from the group too?" The teen decides. If no, the round ends here — no pressure, no exposure.
4. **Group popcorn (only if the leader says yes).** Ask the group, popcorn-style so only those with something to say speak: *"What worked well **for you**? What's one suggestion for them to consider next time?"* The "for you" framing keeps it experiential — how the leading *landed for the listener* — not a verdict on the leader.

**Why it's built this way:** strengths before growth keeps it safe; the leader holds the consent for group feedback, so they keep their agency; popcorn removes the pressure of a forced round; and "for you" language keeps peers reflecting on their own experience rather than grading a friend. This *is* the FC1 formation loop — it's what turned seeing-it into confident-leading in the pilot.

**Timing (confirm with John):** Tier 1 (team) fits the post-session Companion debrief; Tier 2 (group) needs the cohort present, so it runs at session close, right after the teen led. If you'd rather hold both tiers together in one moment at close, say so and I'll write it that way.

## 6. Block-leadability map (🟢 co-lead / 🟡 assist / 🔴 adult-held)

The bright line: teens lead the *container scaffolding and teaching*; adults always hold the *disclosure-bearing center.*

| Wk | Session | Container open/close | Experiential center | Center |
|----|---------|:---:|---------|:---:|
| 1 | Welcome | Adult **models** | First container; "one true sentence" | 🟡 watch |
| 2 | Heart Soil | 🟢 | Heart Soil diagnostic | 🟡 |
| 3 | Telling Your Story | 🟢 | Story circles / blessing round | 🔴 story · 🟢 blessing |
| 4 | Knowing & Being Known | 🟢 | Container-conditions exercise | 🟢 |
| 5 | Safe & Brave | 🟢 | **Confession & restoration** | 🔴 |
| 6 | Hearing God | 🟢 | **PROAPT walk-through** | 🟢 |
| 7 | Garden of Your Heart | 🟢 | Guided walk-through / debrief | 🟡 walk-through · 🔴 debrief |
| 8 | Any Doubts? | 🟢 | Naming private doubts | 🔴 |
| 9 | What Was Prepared | 🟢 | Gifts + downhill mission | 🟢 |
| 10 | Sending | 🟢 | Family commissioning | 🟡 co-lead pieces |

Most of Getting Started *is* the band — because most of it is scripted container-and-teaching. **Garden of Your Heart (7) is split** (John's pilot observation): leading the *guided walk-through* is a simple scripted process a debut senior can run with an adult present, but *catching a heavy disclosure* if one surfaces stays adult-held. Confession (5) and Any Doubts? (8) elicit disclosure more directly and stay fully adult-held. The single safety line: **a young leader may run the process; they never hold what surfaces alone.** That's exactly why the referral reflex (§3 #4) matters most for GotH-type processes in a dorm — where there is no adult in the room to hand it to.

## 7. New role for handbook Section 3

**Companion-in-Formation (FC1 track)** — a discernment-selected, parent-aligned senior teen who co-leads 🟢 blocks with an adult Companion *in the room*, and is being formed toward FC1. Defined alongside the four adult roles, with its limits stated (§8).

## 8. The three rules that never bend — the FC1 limits card

Laminated, in the young leader's hand. Same format as the Crisis Quick-Reference card.

1. **A Companion-in-Formation never counts as one of the two adults.** Supervision is unchanged. "Co-lead" = an adult Companion *in the room, not merely nearby.*
2. **A Companion-in-Formation never takes a disclosure.** If something heavy surfaces, the only job is to hand it to the adult immediately. Not a junior counselor.
3. **Lead only a block you have first received.** See it, then lead it. Never lead a block you haven't experienced as a participant.

**Dorm-context version of the card (post-18):** rules 1–2 become — *you are not the clinician; the moment it's above FC1, you route it.* The card carries a **filled-in referral map** (campus counseling, 988, a pastor, an RA, parents) and the **name of your covering person.** The blank card is not dorm-ready; the *filled* card is.
