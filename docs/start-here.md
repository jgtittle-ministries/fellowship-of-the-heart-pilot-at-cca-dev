# Start here — for the curious

You are at the front door of *Fellowship of the Heart*. This page is the short answer to: **what is this, and how is walking it supposed to bring a person closer to God?**

---

## What this is

*Fellowship of the Heart* (FotH) is what it looks like when a real cohort walks the work of the *Intentional Journey of the Heart* together. Three sequential series, across about a year of Tuesday evenings:

- **[Getting Started](getting-started/index.md)** — ten weeks introducing the four Connects: Self → Others → God → Mission.
- **[Going Deeper](going-deeper/index.md)** — twelve weeks of the harder interior work, and learning to hear God as a body.
- **[Going Out](going-out/index.md)** — twelve weeks of the body sent: into household, vocational, third-place contexts, and sometimes into starting new formation groups.

Plus the [shared participant materials](shared/index.md) — the Personal Heart Journal, the Family Conversation Cards, the Rhythm Card, the Reading List — that walk alongside all three series.

The *Intentional Journey of the Heart* (IJH) sits behind FotH. IJH is the six-volume record of how God's love actually works in a human heart — the operative laws, the diagnostics for where you are, the practices that move you forward. FotH is what those volumes look like when a cohort actually walks them. IJH is the *what* and the *why*; FotH is the *how* and the *with whom*.

The six volumes:

- **Volume 1: Laws of the Spirit** — the 46 Foundational Laws (38 of wide consent — cause-and-effect relationships affirmed across mainstream Christian tradition — plus eight newer ones still being tested), and eight personal Explorations of less-mapped territory. The structural and operational laws underneath the spiritual life.
- **Volume 2: Knowing to Doing** — the diagnostic, therapeutic, and developmental work — the Heart Soil, the Emotional Knots, the Container, the Tool Map, PROAPT, the Four Connects, the Training Plan. FotH is the cohort-walked operational form of Vol 2 most directly.
- **Volume 3: Spiritual Dynamics** — the most speculative of the volumes; attempts to translate Vol 1's structural laws into more formal mathematical and dynamical language. Skippable on a first pass unless you enjoy that frame.
- **Volume 4: Testing Framework** — how to test all of this in real life, at individual, small-group, and larger-study scales.
- **Volume 5: References** — annotated bibliography, the Periodic Table of Spiritual Laws (the catalog organizer), the [Formation Documents](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart-dev/Volume%205.html) (the academic-register companion papers — including MSFIG and the four-part **Hearing-God Papers**, which measure spiritual maturity in hearing God, prescribe its cultivation, and form the heart through the lower and upper levels of internalization), and the operational tool protocols Companions deploy in sessions.
- **Volume 6: Governance** — the framework under which the work is stewarded, extended, and corrected by others beyond the original author. The institutional layer that helps the work outlast its author.

Every claim in IJH carries one of three confidence tiers, named explicitly so you can read with the right posture:

- **Clearly Taught** — multi-author, multi-Testament scriptural support; broadly accepted across mainstream Christian traditions. High-confidence claims you can operate in.
- **Reasonably Inferred** — derivable from scripture by sound reasoning and consistent with experience, but not stated explicitly or widely enough to clear the top tier. Held loosely; needs community testing.
- **Speculative** — interesting enough to pursue, not yet solid enough to build on.

The IJH site is at [jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart/); the [*Read Me First*](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart/read-me-first/) is the substantive front door.

## How walking this brings you closer to God

The work rests on a claim about how formation actually happens.

Most people experience their interior life as noise — feelings, impulses, half-formed prayers, things that *should* be there but aren't. The first thing the journey does is give you words for what is already in your heart: the *Heart Soil* of Mark 4, the *Knots*, the distinction between wound and sin, what the false self is trying to protect. Being able to name your own soil is what makes it possible to receive what God is already doing in it.

From there the journey moves outward in a specific order, and the order matters. You learn to be honestly seen by a small group of other people in a container that is *Safe, Present, Clear,* and *Intentional*. You learn to hear God in scripture as a sustained practice (*PROAPT*) rather than as a duty. You learn to meet God relationally — not just think about Him — through the *Garden of Your Heart*. You face the gap between what you say and what you actually trust (*Any Doubts?*). Eventually, usually in Going Deeper, the cohort starts hearing God *together*, not just individually. That corporate hearing is qualitatively different from individual quiet times, and it is the soil in which real mission grows.

The last movement is *Going Out*. What was prepared beforehand for each of us to walk in (Ephesians 2:10)? Where am I being sent — into my household, my work, the third place I am already in, and sometimes into starting a new formation group of my own? Not everyone is sent to start groups — Acts 13 sent two of the five at Antioch — and the discernment is honest about that. Most are sent back into the lives they already have, but they now know how to be present in them differently.

A word on how to hold all of this. The “laws” of IJH describe how God characteristically works in a human heart — the way creation describes a Maker — not levers that make Him act. Growth is His gift, received by faith, not a technique we run (John 15:5; 1 Cor. 4:7). The four Connects are the shape that grace takes in a life, not a machine that produces closeness. You are not engineering your formation; you are putting yourself where grace flows, in union with Christ, and letting Him do what only He does.
