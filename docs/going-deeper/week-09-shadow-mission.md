## WEEK 9 — THE SHADOW MISSION (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Identify each participant’s primary gift and the shadow direction that gift takes when used wrongly or under stress. Name the shadow as discipline (a thing to watch for and check) not failure (a thing to be ashamed of). Build a working language each participant carries into Wk 10’s calling discernment.

**Anchor scripture.** 1 Corinthians 12:4–11 (gifts varieties); Romans 12:3–8 (gifts in proportion to faith); Matthew 23:1–12 (Jesus naming the Pharisees’ shadow — the same gifts they had, used wrongly).

**Connect focus.** Mission (deepened). The first session of the Mission-deepened block. Wk 10 (Acts 13 calling) and Wk 11 (cohort assessment) build on tonight’s shadow work.

**Mode.** Shared teaching of gifts and shadow (19 min). Cohort circles for the standing-pair shadow conversation (38 min). Brief shared merge for voluntary public namings (8 min).

**Center.** With the standing pair, each participant identifies one primary gift and its specific shadow direction. The shadow is named in the participant’s own life — where the gift has actually drifted, in this season, toward harm. Pair partners hold the naming as discipline; the Cohort Companion floats.

**Between-session practice.** Daily noticing of the shadow when it surfaces — not to fight it, to name it. ‘There it is again’ is the practice. Standing-pair check-in this week is explicit: ‘Where did the shadow show up; what did you name when it did?’

**IJH source.** Vol 5 Mission-Arc Family (A30 Shadow Mission Protocol — the disowned alternative trajectory brought into the light); Vol 1 Exp 1 on hearing as the discriminator between gift and shadow; the Pharisee analysis from Matt 23 as the central case of gift used wrongly.

## WATCH FOR (Week 9 specific risks — read twice)

*The shadow is dangerous when unnamed and dangerous when named the wrong way. Tonight’s architecture distinguishes naming-as-discipline (helpful, lifelong) from naming-as-identity (‘I am my shadow’, harmful) and from naming-as-self-flagellation (recurring guilt without growth, harmful). Read these risks twice; the room can tip in any of these directions if the team is not attentive.*

**Identifying with the shadow. ‘I am the controlling one.’ ‘I am the people-pleaser.’ The shadow is not the participant. It is a direction the gift takes wrongly under stress or when discipline lapses. Re-frame: ‘Your gift is X; the shadow direction is Y; you are not Y.’**

**Self-flagellation. The naming becomes another round of ‘I am bad and need to do better.’ The architecture is observation, not condemnation. ‘There it is again’ is the discipline; ‘I am terrible for letting it happen again’ is not.**

**Naming someone else’s shadow. ‘My pair partner’s shadow is X.’ Cohort Companion stops this immediately. The shadow is named in YOUR own life, by YOU, with YOUR pair partner witnessing. The pair partner does not interpret.**

**The teen-parent across cohorts naming each other’s shadow at home. The shadow named tonight in the cohort STAYS in the cohort. Family members do not deploy it as a corrective at home this week. Pastoral 1:1 if the dynamic is acute.**

**The participant who claims to have no shadow. ‘My gift hasn’t really turned wrong.’ Possible, but rare. The Cohort Companion gently invites: ‘Where does your gift get tired? Where does it overreach? When you are stressed, where does its energy go?’ The shadow shows up under stress.**

**The participant who names the shadow theatrically. ‘My shadow is that I am completely manipulative.’ Cohort Companion pushes for specificity: ‘What’s the smaller, more specific version? Where, with whom, in what kind of situation, has the gift turned in this direction recently?’**

**The participant whose shadow named tonight is genuinely dangerous to others around them (manipulation, controlling behavior toward a child or spouse, exploitation of authority). Receive without alarm. Pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours; the shadow naming is the threshold of the work, not the conclusion. Section 6 protocol if patterns crossed thresholds in the past.**

**The Pharisee parallel mishandled. The teaching uses Matt 23 because the Pharisees had real gifts — teaching, devotion to the Word, moral seriousness — and Jesus named where the same gifts had turned. The teaching is not ‘the Pharisees were the bad guys.’ The teaching is ‘the same gifts you have can turn the same way; the naming is the prevention.’**

**Generational projection. A parent’s shadow analysis becomes ‘kids these days have a shadow of\_\_\_\_\_.’ A senior teen’s shadow analysis becomes ‘parents have a shadow of\_\_\_\_\_.’ The shadow is your OWN, in your OWN life, with your OWN pair. Cohort Companion redirects.**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 9)

*Crisis risk is moderate. The shadow naming surfaces specific patterns of harm; some patterns will be larger than the pair can hold and require immediate pastoral or clinical engagement.*

**If a named shadow reveals a pattern of harm that is currently active toward another person (a child, a spouse, a colleague). Cohort Companion: ‘What you just named is real and is bigger than tonight’s pair conversation. We are going to walk this with you starting tomorrow.’ Pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours; clinical referral as appropriate; mandatory-reporting protocols if applicable.**

**If a named shadow reveals a pattern that the participant has been hiding for years and the naming is heavier than expected. Receive without rushing. Cohort Companion stays with the pair after the cohort circle ends; Lead Companion follow-up within 48 hours.**

**If the pair partner becomes activated by what was named (because the partner’s own shadow is similar, or because the named pattern has affected them historically). Cohort Companion stays with the activated partner; another Cohort Companion can take over the listening role.**

**If a teen names a shadow that suggests current self-harm, suicidality, or harm to others. Section 6 protocol. Two-adult rule. Mandatory-reporting law applies as relevant.**

**If a participant becomes flooded mid-conversation. Same protocol as Wks 3 and 5. Cohort Companion stays.**

**If two participants in different cohorts name shadows that converge on each other (a parent’s controlling shadow + their teen’s avoidant shadow). The Cohort Companions do not name this to either side in the cohorts. Pastoral 1:1 with both within the week; family-level conversation only with explicit consent and pastoral support.**

**Default. Section 6 of the Going Deeper Handbook. Pastoral / clinical backup confirmed by name and number.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

The IJH framework, especially Vol 4, names the shadow as the under-recognized dynamic of mission and discipleship. Every gift has a shadow direction — the same gift used wrongly, or the same energy turned the wrong way under stress. The shadow is not the absence of the gift; it is the gift turned in on the user or on the people the gift was meant to serve. The teacher’s gift, in shadow, becomes condescension. The administrator’s gift, in shadow, becomes control. The mercy-shower’s gift, in shadow, becomes enabling. The prophet’s gift, in shadow, becomes condemnation. Same energy; wrong direction.

Matthew 23 is the central case. Jesus, in the most explicit address to the Pharisees recorded in the Gospels, names where their gifts have turned. He does not deny their gifts — ‘they sit on Moses’ seat’ — he names where the gifts have become weight on the people they were called to serve. The teaching gift turned into burdens not lifted by a finger. The devotion-to-the-Word gift turned into majoring on minors (mint, dill, cumin) while neglecting the weightier matters (justice, mercy, faithfulness). The moral-seriousness gift turned into the cleaning of cup-exteriors while interiors remained full of greed. Same gifts the Pharisees actually had. Wrong direction.

Tonight’s frame: each of us has gifts; each gift has a shadow direction; the shadow shows up under stress, when discipline lapses, when the gift is used in service of self instead of in service of God or others. Naming the shadow in advance — specifically, in our own life, in this season — makes it nameable when it shows up real-time. ‘There it is again’ is the lifelong discipline; ‘I am the controlling one’ is identity formation around the shadow and is harmful.

This is also the first session of the Mission-deepened block. The God block (Wks 6, 7, 8) deepened our hearing. The Mission block (Wks 9 and 10) tests where each of us comes most alive (gift) and where the same energy turns wrong (shadow). The shadow work tonight is preparation for downhill mission — Vol 4’s distinction between mission as the natural overflow of what the Spirit has formed (downhill) and mission as effortful self-direction (uphill). Going Deeper forms the conditions for downhill mission to emerge; Going Out engages it directly. Wk 10’s Acts 13 calling discernment for one cohort member depends on tonight’s shadow naming — the cohort cannot well discern a calling for someone whose gifts they cannot name and whose shadow they have not heard.

From Vol 1 Exp 1: hearing is the discriminator between gift and shadow. The same person, on the same day, can speak in their gift or in their shadow; the difference is whether the speaking comes from listening to the Spirit first or from anxiety, ego, or unmanaged energy. The hearing chain (Wk 6) and the naming of the shadow (tonight) work together: better hearing produces better gift-discernment in the moment; named shadow produces faster recognition when the gift drifts.

**Pre-Work for the Companion Team (this week)**

**Personal pre-work**

Each Companion runs the gift-and-shadow diagnostic on themselves this week. Forty-five minutes alone. The team that has named its own shadow can hold the cohort’s shadow naming; the team that has not will struggle.

First — name your primary gift. Specifically. ‘Teaching, especially the moment of helping someone see something they could not see before.’ ‘Hospitality, specifically the work of making people feel welcomed in a room they were nervous about entering.’ ‘Administration, the gift of seeing the structure that makes a thing actually work and putting it in place.’ Specific.

Second — name where that gift has drifted in your life this season. The shadow direction. Be specific. ‘My teaching gift has drifted toward correcting people I disagree with rather than serving people who want to learn.’ ‘My hospitality has drifted toward managing the anxiety I have about the room rather than welcoming the people in it.’ ‘My administration has drifted toward control — needing things done my way rather than well.’ Specific.

Third — name the discipline. Not ‘I will stop having a shadow.’ You will not stop. The shadow is the cost of having the gift; the discipline is naming it when it surfaces. ‘When I notice myself correcting rather than serving, I will name it: there it is again. Then I will choose whether to continue or shift.’ The naming is the discipline.

Bring all three pieces — your gift, your specific shadow, your discipline — to the team meeting. The team is going to be in cohort circles tonight modeling this for participants; rehearse it now.

## Script (the read)

*“Three passages tonight. Listen to all three.”*

**Teaching points (use any format — do not read this verbatim)**

**•** Three passages, three different angles. 1 Corinthians 12 names varieties of gifts from the same Spirit. Romans 12 reminds us to think with sober judgment about our gifts — not too highly, not too low. And Matthew 23 names what happens to gifts when they turn.

**•** Notice what Jesus does in Matt 23. He does not deny the Pharisees’ gifts. ‘They sit on Moses’ seat’ — they have authority; the people should listen to what they teach. The gifts are real. What Jesus names is the direction the gifts have taken — ‘the works they do.’ The teaching gift turned into burdens not lifted. The devotion-to-Word gift turned into majoring on minors. The moral-seriousness gift turned into cup-cleaning while interiors stayed full of greed. Same gifts. Wrong direction.

**•** This is the working frame for tonight. Your gift is not the question. The question is the direction it has taken in your life this season — specifically, recently, with whom and where.

## The four-by-four — major gift archetypes and shadow directions

**TEACHER (the gift of helping people see what they could not see) → SHADOW: condescension. The same gift that opens vision can become correction-as-superiority. ‘Let me explain to you why you are wrong’ instead of ‘let me serve you in seeing.’**

**SHEPHERD (the gift of caring for people, especially those in pain) → SHADOW: enabling. The same gift that protects can keep people from facing what they need to face. ‘I will smooth this for you’ instead of ‘I will walk with you through this.’**

**PROPHET (the gift of seeing what is true and naming it) → SHADOW: condemnation. The same gift that names truth can become naming-with-no-mercy. ‘This is wrong and you are wrong’ instead of ‘this is wrong and the Father is restoring it.’**

**SERVANT-ADMIN (the gift of seeing structure and putting it in place) → SHADOW: control. The same gift that makes things actually work can become needing-things-my-way. ‘This must be done correctly’ instead of ‘let us serve the work together.’**

*These four are not exhaustive. Most participants will recognize themselves in one primary, with secondary gifts in the other three. The shadow direction is the danger of the primary gift, especially under stress.*

**How to find your shadow**

## Where does my gift get tired? Tired-gift drifts toward shadow.

**Where does my gift overreach? Overreach is the gift trying to do something it isn’t for; the overreach is shadow.**

**When I am stressed, where does my gift’s energy go? Stress is the test; the shadow direction is the stress destination.**

**Where, with whom, in what kind of situation, has my gift turned wrongly recently? Specific. Not ‘I sometimes correct people.’ ‘Last Friday with my colleague when she pushed back on my presentation, I corrected her in front of the team in a way that was about my ego, not the work.’**

**Block 5 — Standing-Pair Shadow Conversation (7:36–8:14, 38 min)**

Each cohort circle splits into the standing pairs. The Cohort Companion floats. Pairs sit knee-to-knee with at least 6 feet of buffer.

**Inside the cohort circle — Companion script**

## Opening (2 min)

*“Thirty-eight minutes. Eighteen each direction; two for protocol signing.”*

*“First: read H9.3 with your pair partner. Sign it; exchange. The protocol governs the architecture for tonight’s pair work.”*

*“Rhythm. Person A names their primary gift, where it has drifted recently — specifically — and the discipline they want to practice when the shadow shows up. About 14 minutes. Person B listens, no interpretation, asks one clarifying question if it would help, and at the end witnesses the naming: ‘I heard that your gift is X, the shadow direction in this season is Y, and the discipline is Z. I receive it as honest data.’ About 4 minutes for clarifying and witness. Then we switch.”*

*“If you are not yet sure which gift is primary, that is honest. The pair conversation can help name it; sometimes the gift becomes clear when the shadow shows up first — you trace it backward.”*

*“Decide who goes first. The other partner can begin reviewing H9.1 silently while their partner prepares.”*

## How each direction runs (≈18 min per direction)

**Person A names their primary gift. Specifically. ‘My gift is teaching, especially the moment of helping someone see something they could not see before.’ Or: ‘I am still discerning between teaching and shepherding; here is what I know.’ Either is honest. (3–4 min)**

**Person A names where the gift has drifted in this season. Specifically. ‘Recently with [colleague], when [situation], I corrected rather than served. The energy of teaching turned into condescension because [trigger].’ (5–7 min)**

**Person A names the discipline they want to practice when the shadow shows up. ‘When I notice the corrective impulse rising, I will name it: there it is again. Then I will choose whether to continue or shift.’ (2–3 min)**

**Person B asks ONE clarifying question if it would help (‘what was the trigger’, ‘is the gift teaching or coaching’). Not interpretation. (1 min, often skipped)**

**Person B witnesses the naming. ‘I heard that your gift is X, the shadow direction in this season is Y, and the discipline is Z. I receive it as honest data. I will pray for you in the discipline this week.’ (1–2 min)**

**Brief silence. (30 sec)**

**At the switch signal, the directions reverse.**

**Cohort Companion: when to intervene**

**•** If a participant cannot find their gift — affirm. ‘Where do you come most alive when serving? Where have people thanked you for what you did? What kind of work makes you feel like you were made for this?’ The pair conversation may produce the answer the worksheet alone could not.

**•** If a participant’s shadow is generic (‘I am sometimes prideful’) — push for specificity: ‘Where, with whom, recently, did your gift turn wrongly? Specifically.’

**•** If a participant is identifying with the shadow (‘I am the controlling one’) — re-frame: ‘Your gift is administration; the shadow direction is control; you are not control.’

**•** If a pair partner is interpreting rather than witnessing (‘I think your shadow is actually X’) — redirect: ‘Listen and witness. The shadow is named by the person whose shadow it is.’

**•** If a participant is performing self-flagellation (‘I am a terrible person, my shadow is everywhere’) — re-frame: ‘The architecture is observation, not condemnation. “There it is again” is the discipline; “I am terrible” is not.’

**•** If a named shadow reveals an active pattern of harm to another person — Cohort Companion stays with the pair after the cohort circle. Pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours.

**•** Watch the 18-minute switch. The symmetry is non-negotiable. If the first direction ran long, the second still gets its 18 minutes.

**Handout H9.1 — Gifts and Shadow Reference**

*From the IJH Mission-Arc Family (Vol 5 — see A30 Shadow Mission Protocol for the shadow piece). Carry this card; the gift-and-shadow language is the working frame for Wk 10’s calling discernment and beyond.*

**The four major gift archetypes**

## Each gift has a characteristic energy and a characteristic shadow direction

**TEACHER — the gift of helping people see what they could not see before. Energy: revealing, opening, naming. Shadow: condescension. The same gift that opens vision can become correction-as-superiority.**

**SHEPHERD — the gift of caring for people, especially those in pain. Energy: protecting, comforting, walking-with. Shadow: enabling. The same gift that protects can keep people from facing what they need to face.**

**PROPHET — the gift of seeing what is true and naming it. Energy: discerning, declaring, calling. Shadow: condemnation. The same gift that names truth can become naming-with-no-mercy.**

**SERVANT-ADMIN — the gift of seeing structure and putting it in place. Energy: organizing, executing, completing. Shadow: control. The same gift that makes things work can become needing-things-my-way.**

**How to find your gift**

## Where do I come most alive when serving?

**Where have people thanked me, specifically, for what I did or who I was?**

**What kind of work makes me feel like I was made for this?**

**If I have to choose between teaching, shepherding, prophesying, and administering — which one is most natural to me, even if I am not best at it?**

**How to find your shadow**

## Where does my gift get tired?

**Where does my gift overreach?**

**When I am stressed, where does my gift’s energy go?**

**Where, with whom, in what kind of situation, has my gift turned wrongly recently?**

**The Pharisee case (Matthew 23)**

## Jesus does not deny the Pharisees’ gifts. ‘They sit on Moses’ seat’ — they have authority; the people should listen to what they teach.

**Jesus names where the gifts have turned. The teaching gift turned into burdens not lifted. The devotion-to-Word gift turned into majoring on minors. The moral-seriousness gift turned into cup-cleaning while interiors stayed full of greed.**

**The lesson is not ‘the Pharisees were the bad guys.’ The lesson is ‘the same gifts you have can turn the same way; the naming is the prevention.’**

**The shadow is named not to fight but to notice. ‘There it is again’ is the lifelong discipline.**

**How the noticing works**

## The shadow surfaces in real time. A conversation, a moment of stress, a meeting, a parenting interaction. You notice the energy moving in the wrong direction — the corrective impulse rising, the controlling impulse rising, the rescuing impulse rising, the condemning impulse rising.

**Name it internally. ‘There it is again.’ No drama. No additional self-criticism. No ‘I am terrible.’ Just the noticing.**

**Choose. Sometimes you continue in the shadow direction with awareness; sometimes you shift; sometimes you pause briefly to recover. The noticing makes the choice possible. The energy unnoticed is the energy unmanaged.**

**Across weeks, the noticing speeds up. What used to take 20 minutes to recognize starts taking 5 minutes, then 30 seconds, then real-time. That is the discipline maturing.**
