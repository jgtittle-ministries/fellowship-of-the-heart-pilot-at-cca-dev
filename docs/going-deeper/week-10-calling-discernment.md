## WEEK 10 — CALLING DISCERNMENT, ACTS 13 (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Enact the Antioch paradigm for ONE pre-briefed cohort member (the Discerner) whose emerging calling is at a discernment point. The body fasts (Wk 10 has the team’s pre-fast), prays, listens, surfaces what was heard, and — if the discernment converges — lays hands and sends. The architecture is Group Level 5; most cohorts attempt Level 5 in this single moment and operate at Level 4 most of the time.

**Anchor scripture.** Acts 13:1–3 (Antioch — the prophets and teachers worshiping, fasting, hearing the Spirit, laying hands, sending). Acts 15:28 (‘it has seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us’ — the Jerusalem Council pattern of corporate decision).

**Connect focus.** Mission (deepened) — apex. The work that the entirety of Going Deeper has built toward, for ONE member tonight. The cohort’s capacity to do this work for one member is itself a sign of formation maturity.

**Mode.** Whole-room. No cohort split. The whole cohort sits as one circle with the Discerner’s chair in the centre, similar to Wk 7’s geometry but with longer listening time and a possible commissioning moment.

**Center.** The Discerner’s emerging calling. NOT ‘what should I do with my life’ — a specific direction the Discerner has been sensing (a job, a ministry, a place, a role, a specific setting-apart). The cohort’s job is to listen FOR the Discerner; speak back humbly what they heard; weigh together with the Discerner whether the Spirit is confirming or correcting. If the discernment converges, the cohort may lay hands and bless the sending.

**Between-session practice.** Each member journals what came to them for the Discerner that they did not speak aloud. The Discerner journals daily across a longer integration window — typically 4–6 weeks — because callings are walked into, not decided in 90 minutes. Standing-pair check-in this week is explicit: ‘What did the cohort hear for the Discerner, and what is it teaching us about our own calling?’

**IJH source.** Vol 4 (calling and mission); MSFIG (5-level taxonomy — Level 5 is apostolic team that sends, the architectural target tonight, 70% confidence); Vol 1 Exp 1 (corporate hearing for a specific member); Acts 13 as the central New Testament case.

## WATCH FOR (Week 10 specific risks — read twice)

*Wk 10 is the highest-stakes session of the entirety of Going Deeper structurally. The risks are spiritual (false confirmation of a calling that is not yet clear; cohort enthusiasm pushing the Discerner where the Spirit is not), pastoral (the Discerner’s family or current role being upended by what surfaces), and architectural (treating the laying-on-of-hands as ceremony rather than confirmation). Read these twice.*

**False confirmation. The cohort is naturally inclined toward affirmation; the Discerner’s emerging calling tends to attract ‘yes, do it’ contributions even when the Spirit may be saying ‘wait’ or ‘not this direction.’ The Lead Companion weighs explicitly per 1 Cor 14:29 and is willing to name when the cohort’s contributions converge on cohort enthusiasm rather than Spirit confirmation.**

**Cohort coercion of the Discerner. ‘God told me you should \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘We are sensing strongly that you must \_\_\_\_\_.’ The Lead Companion stops this immediately. Acts 13 has the Spirit speaking ‘set apart for me Barnabas and Saul’ — directive in form but spoken to the body, then weighed by the body. Coercive contributions to a single Discerner are not the architecture.**

**Premature commissioning. The laying-on-of-hands moment is contingent on the discernment converging tonight. If it does not converge, there is no commissioning; the Discerner integrates over weeks; there may or may not be a future commissioning. Resist the social pressure to commission anyway because everyone showed up.**

**Calling-as-vocation collapse. The Discerner may bring a calling that is vocational (a job, a degree, a ministry); some Discerners bring callings that are about who they are becoming (a role in their family, a posture in their existing life, a kind of presence). Both are valid. The cohort listens for whatever the Discerner brings, not for what the cohort thinks a calling should be.**

**The Discerner who shifts the question mid-session. Possible. ‘What I came in to discern is shifting as the cohort listens.’ Honour. The Lead Companion may invite a brief pause in the surfacing for the Discerner to re-frame. Sometimes the listening reveals that the original question was the wrong question.**

**Family-across-cohorts implications. If the Discerner’s calling has direct family implications (a teen choosing a college far from home; a parent considering a vocational change that affects the family; a parent considering a ministry that pulls from family time), pastoral 1:1 with all family members across cohorts within 48 hours.**

**The cohort hearing nothing for the Discerner. Possible and honest. ‘I listened; nothing specific surfaced for [Discerner’s name].’ The Lead Companion affirms; the Discerner integrates the cohort’s honest silence as data. Hearing nothing is not the cohort failing the Discerner; it may be the Spirit’s timing.**

**The Discerner’s calling intersecting cohort-as-body issues. ‘My calling involves staying in this church / leaving this church / starting this thing.’ The cohort cannot adjudicate organizational decisions about itself or its broader church on the spot. Receive without imposing the cohort’s organizational preferences.**

**Last-week-of-substantive-work fatigue. Wk 11 is integration; Wk 12 is sending. Wk 10 is the last session where the cohort does substantive new formation work together. Some members will arrive tired; some will arrive with their own end-of-Going-Deeper material surfacing. The team holds the room around the Discerner without diluting the focus.**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 10)

*Crisis risk is moderate to high. Pastoral / clinical backup confirmed by name and number for the night. Two-call protocol active.*

**If the Discerner becomes flooded mid-pre-frame or mid-receiving. Pause the architecture. Lead Companion stays with the Discerner; Cohort Companion holds the room. Brief offer of a step-out. The Discerner can choose to continue, postpone (the cohort closes the session with a different shape), or step out and integrate privately. Do not push through.**

**If a contribution from the cohort surfaces material that the Discerner experiences as activating (a memory, a fear, a previously unnamed loss). Receive without rushing. Lead Companion: ‘What just surfaced is real and may be part of what the Spirit is doing in your discernment. Hold it; we will integrate.’ Pastoral 1:1 within 48 hours.**

**If the cohort’s contributions converge on a direction the Discerner is currently ambivalent about and the convergence becomes pressure. Lead Companion names: ‘What we are hearing has a clear direction. The Discerner is the one who weighs and walks. Tonight we are honest about what surfaced; we are not deciding for the Discerner.’ No commissioning if the Discerner is not ready.**

**If the Discerner’s calling involves the dissolution of an existing relationship (marriage, ministry, employment) and the cohort’s listening confirms a direction that has profound consequences. Pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours; clinical referral as appropriate; no rushed action across the integration weeks.**

**If the Discerner’s calling involves leaving the cohort’s church or community. Receive humbly. The cohort cannot retain a calling that is sending. Pastoral support for the Discerner; pastoral support for the broader community as the Discerner integrates.**

**If a teen Discerner’s calling involves significant separation from family (college far away, a ministry plan parents are concerned about). The teen’s parent in another cohort hears it in real time. Pre-handled per the team pre-meet protocol; pastoral 1:1 with the family within 48 hours.**

**If after the listening and surfacing, the cohort’s contributions converge on the Discerner stopping or postponing the calling rather than walking into it. Lead Companion names this honestly. ‘What we heard was the Spirit’s confirmation that this direction is not now; the Discerner integrates this with weight.’ Pastoral 1:1; the Discerner is not abandoned because the calling did not get confirmed.**

**Default. Section 6 of the Going Deeper Handbook covers anything that crosses the safety threshold.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

Acts 13:1–3 names what is, structurally, the most significant moment in the establishment of the Gentile mission. The church at Antioch had a small group of prophets and teachers — Barnabas, Simeon called Niger, Lucius of Cyrene, Manaen (a member of the court of Herod the tetrarch), and Saul. Five named people. They were worshiping and fasting; the Spirit said ‘set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them’; the body fasted and prayed; they laid hands on them and sent them off.

From Vol 4 (and the MSFIG group taxonomy): this is Level 5 group formation. A body that hears the Spirit speak about specific members of the body, weighs what was heard, and acts on it. Acts 13 is the New Testament archetype of corporate calling discernment. Acts 15 (the Jerusalem Council) is the related case where the body discerns a doctrinal question through the same architecture — they listened to what God had been doing, weighed scripture, and concluded ‘it has seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us.’ Both texts assume the body has the capacity to discern together.

Wk 10 attempts the Antioch architecture for ONE cohort member — the Discerner — whose emerging calling is at a discernment point. The pilot cohort is unlikely to be at Level 5 sustainably; we are attempting it tonight for one specific moment. The honest aim is to enact the architecture, with humility about what we can produce — and to give the Discerner a real corporate moment of discernment that will inform their integration over the following weeks.

The choice of Discerner is significant. This is the highest-stakes volunteer slot in Going Deeper. Two protocols for selecting the Discerner are described in Pre-Work below; the team picks the one that fits the pilot cohort and the specific Discerners available.

The whole of Going Deeper has built toward tonight. Wks 2–3 named what is in the way (soils, knots, lies). Wks 4–5 cleared the relational and confessional channels (co-processing, confession). Wks 6–8 deepened the hearing (PROAPT, corporate listening prayer for one Bringer’s question, the cohort hearing about itself). Wk 9 surfaced gifts and shadows. Tonight the cohort puts all of it to work for one member’s calling. The Discerner walks into the integration weeks with what the body heard; the cohort walks into Wks 11–12 with the formation maturity that doing this work has produced.

**Pre-Work for the Companion Team (this week)**

**Personal pre-work — Companion fast**

The Antioch pattern in Acts 13:1–3 has the prophets and teachers fasting before the Spirit speaks. The team fasts in some form during the week before Wk 10. Each Companion chooses an honest fast: a meal a day for three days, a 24-hour fast on the day before Wk 10, a fast from a specific habit (caffeine, social media, news) for the week. The fast is real but right-sized; medical conditions and dietary realities are respected. The point is not asceticism; the point is the bodily posture of expectancy: the body is asking the Spirit to speak, and the body is ordering its appetites in a way that makes hearing easier.

Each Companion also commits to specific intercessory prayer for the Discerner across the week. Specifically: by name, by the calling the Discerner is bringing, by the cohort’s capacity to listen well. The team’s prayer is part of the architecture; without it the room will be doing its own work tonight.

Each Companion reads Acts 13 in full at least twice across the week. Notice Antioch as a body — a small, multi-ethnic, multi-class formation team that had been doing real work for some time before this moment. Notice also that Saul and Barnabas were already known to the body before they were set apart; the calling did not come out of nowhere.

**Discerner selection — TWO protocols, team picks ONE**

Wk 10 needs ONE pre-briefed Discerner. The team uses one of the two protocols below. The choice is made at the team pre-meet 48 hours before Wk 9, so the Discerner can be selected, contacted, and pre-briefed across the Wk 9-to-Wk 10 week.

## PROTOCOL A — Team identification (default for first-pilot cohorts)

**The Cohort Companions identify, at the Wk 9 pre-meet, one or two prospective Discerners per cohort whose emerging calling is at a discernment point. Same five readiness signals as the Wk 7 Bringer Selection Worksheet (see Addenda Section E), but with two additional Wk 10-specific signals:**

**ADDITIONAL SIGNAL ONE — the calling is real and currently at a decision-point, not vague or future. The Discerner is sensing a specific direction in the next 3–9 months: a job change, a degree, a ministry, a place, a specific setting-apart. ‘I might do something different someday’ is not Wk 10.**

**ADDITIONAL SIGNAL TWO — the cohort can hold what surfaces. The Discerner’s calling can be received by the cohort without significant relational rupture among other members. A teen Discerner whose calling involves leaving home far away — this is real and the cohort can hold it. A parent Discerner whose calling is a marital separation that has not yet been disclosed — this is bigger than the cohort can hold and pastoral support is the architecture, not corporate listening prayer.**

**The team selects ONE Discerner across the three cohorts. The Cohort Companion who knows the Discerner best holds primary discernment. The selected Discerner is contacted by Saturday with the invitation; pre-brief by Monday evening; final confirmation Tuesday morning.**

## PROTOCOL B — Self-nomination via Volunteer Application (advanced or returning cohorts)

**At the close of Wk 8, the Lead Companion names that Wk 10 is the Acts 13 calling discernment for one cohort member, and that participants whose emerging calling is at a discernment point are invited to apply. The application is on H10.5 (in the Wk 10 handouts pack but distributed in advance for this protocol).**

**Applications are submitted to the Lead Companion privately by the Sunday after Wk 8. The team triages applications at the Wk 9 pre-meet. Same five Bringer signals plus the two Wk 10-specific signals; same selection process; same pre-brief timing.**

**Self-nomination has two advantages over team identification: it surfaces calling-discernment material the team may not be aware of (a participant whose external life looks settled but whose calling discernment is acute); it preserves the participant’s agency in volunteering for a public, vulnerable role.**

**Self-nomination has two disadvantages: it can attract a Discerner whose self-volunteering reflects need-for-attention more than calling readiness; it can produce more applications than the team can triage well in 48 hours.**

**Recommended use: pilots with first-time cohorts default to Protocol A; pilots with returning participants from prior fall cohorts may use Protocol B because the team’s familiarity with the cohort is lower at the start of the second cohort’s fall. Adapt by pilot.**

**Block 3 — Acts 13 and the Antioch Architecture (7:13–7:30, 17 min)**

## Script (the read)

*“Tonight’s anchor scripture is Acts 13:1–3. Listen.”*

*(Read Acts 13:1–3 from the physical Bible, slowly.)*

*Now there were in the church at Antioch prophets and teachers, Barnabas, Simeon who was called Niger, Lucius of Cyrene, Manaen a member of the court of Herod the tetrarch, and Saul. While they were worshiping the Lord and fasting, the Holy Spirit said, ‘Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them.’ Then after fasting and praying they laid their hands on them and sent them off.*

*— Acts 13:1–3 (ESV)*

**Teaching points (use any format — do not read this verbatim)**

**•** Five named people. A small body. Multi-ethnic, multi-class. Barnabas the Cypriot, Simeon called Niger (‘the black’), Lucius of Cyrene, Manaen who was raised in Herod’s court, Saul of Tarsus. They had been doing real work together for some time. The Antioch body was not a brand-new group; the formation had time to settle.

**•** Worshiping and fasting. The body had been ordering its life around expectancy of the Spirit. The fast is not magic; it is bodily posture — the body saying ‘we are listening; we are not running on appetite tonight.’ Our team has fasted in some form across this week for the same reason.

**•** ‘The Holy Spirit said.’ The text is matter-of-fact about the speaking. We do not know if the Spirit spoke through one prophet, multiple prophets, an internal sense in the room, an external utterance. What we know: the Spirit spoke; the body heard; the body responded.

**•** ‘Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul.’ Specific names. The Spirit specifies. The discernment is not generic; it is calling-specific. Barnabas and Saul, not Manaen and Simeon. The cohort listens for specificity tonight.

**•** ‘For the work to which I have called them.’ Past tense. The calling preceded the body’s discernment. Saul and Barnabas had been being called by the Spirit before the body ratified what the Spirit had been doing. The body’s job is to recognize what the Spirit has been doing in the Discerner, not to invent a new calling.

**•** ‘They laid their hands on them and sent them off.’ The body acted. The discernment was not abstract; it produced specific commissioning and specific sending. Tonight we may or may not reach a commissioning moment; if we do, it is the body acting on what the body discerned, not a ceremony added at the end.

## The four-element Antioch architecture

**WORSHIP + FAST — the body’s posture before listening. The team has lived this across the week.**

**SPIRIT SPEAKS — specific guidance about specific people. We listen tonight for what the Spirit has been doing in the Discerner.**

**BODY LAYS HANDS — the body confirms what the Spirit has spoken. Contingent on convergence; not automatic.**

**BODY SENDS — the discernment produces specific action. The Discerner walks into the integration weeks; if convergence emerged, the cohort has blessed the walking.**

*“Tonight we attempt this for [Discerner’s name]. The Spirit may speak; the body may converge; the laying-on-of-hands may happen. Or convergence may not emerge tonight, and the Discerner integrates over weeks with what was honestly heard. Both are real.”*

**Block 4 — Discerner Pre-Frames the Calling (7:30–7:38, 8 min)**

The Discerner moves to the centre chair. The Lead Companion takes the chair beside them. The room re-orients to face the centre.

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“[Discerner’s name] has agreed to bring their emerging calling into the centre tonight. They have been pre-briefed; the calling is right-sized for this architecture. They will name what they are sensing, the discernment point they are at, and what they are seeking from the body. The cohort will then listen.”*

*“[Discerner’s name], take a breath. The room is with you. In your own words, the calling and the discernment point.”*

## How the Discerner pre-frames (≈7 min)

**The Discerner names the calling. Specifically. ‘I am sensing a call to \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘For the past three months I have been feeling that the Spirit is setting me apart for \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘A specific direction has been clarifying — \_\_\_\_\_.’ (2–3 min)**

**The Discerner names the discernment point. Where they are now. ‘I am between two specific options.’ ‘I am sensing a direction but I do not know if it is from the Spirit or from my own preference.’ ‘I have a sense that I am being asked to walk into \_\_\_\_\_ but I cannot yet hear with confidence.’ (2–3 min)**

**The Discerner names what they are seeking from the body. ‘I am asking the cohort to listen for whether this rings true.’ ‘I am asking for any specific impressions about what the Spirit may be saying.’ ‘I am asking the cohort to hold what surfaces — confirmation, correction, redirection, or silence.’ (1–2 min)**

**The Discerner names any specific contexts the body needs to know. Family implications; vocational implications; what they have already discerned with their standing pair and the team; what they have not yet discerned. (1 min)**

**The Discerner stops. Takes a breath. Looks around the circle briefly.**

Watch for: the Discerner extending past 8 minutes. The Lead Companion gently lands the pre-frame at 7:38. The discipline is for the Discerner to bring what they have prepared, not to over-explain.

**Block 5 — Silent Intercession + Listening for the Discerner (7:38–8:00, 22 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“Twenty-two minutes. We listen for [Discerner’s name]. Each of us in our own posture — prayer, intercession, listening for the Spirit’s word about this calling, this person, this discernment point.”*

*“The disciplines: hold [Discerner’s name] in mind by name; listen for the Spirit; when something surfaces, mark it briefly on H10.3; if your own material surfaces, set it aside and return to listening for [Discerner].”*

*“If nothing surfaces, that is honest. Listening without speaking is part of the body’s discernment. Some members will hear; some will not.”*

*“Twenty-two minutes. Begin.”*

## During the silence (22 min)

**Hold the FULL twenty-two minutes. The longer silence is part of what produces real hearing for a calling. Do not shorten because the room feels heavy.**

**The Discerner sits in the centre chair, eyes open or closed as they choose. The Discerner is not performing presence; they are receiving. If the Discerner becomes flooded, Lead Companion quietly checks at the side of the chair.**

**The Lead Companion listens too. The team’s hearing is part of the body’s hearing.**

**Watch for any participant who is visibly distressed. Cohort Companions float visibly but quietly.**

**At about 12 min, gentle reminder if needed: ‘Halfway. Stay with [Discerner’s name].’ Most cohorts do not need this; some do.**

**At 22 min, quiet bell or named close: ‘Time. Take a breath together.’**

**Block 6 — Surfacing What the Cohort Heard for the Discerner (8:00–8:15, 15 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“Welcome back. Around the circle. ONE thing each of you heard FOR [Discerner’s name] during the silence. Brief; 30 to 60 seconds. Pass anytime.”*

*“Speak humbly. ‘I heard,’ ‘I sensed,’ ‘what came to me was.’ NOT ‘God says you should.’ The discernment belongs to [Discerner’s name] in the Spirit’s presence; we are surfacing what we heard, not directing.”*

*“If you heard nothing for [Discerner’s name], say so. ‘I listened; nothing specific surfaced.’ That is honest. Hearing nothing is part of the body’s discernment.”*

*“I will capture each contribution in my notebook. I will start; one of the Cohort Companions will go second. [Discerner’s name], your job is to receive each contribution — weight will come later. You may take notes; you do not respond yet.”*

## How surfacing runs (≈14 min for content)

**Lead Companion goes first. ONE specific thing they heard for the Discerner.**

**One Cohort Companion goes second. Same architecture.**

**Around the circle. Each member 30–60 seconds. Pass anytime. With 24 people that is 12–20 min; the contribution length adjusts to fit.**

**Lead Companion captures every contribution in a notebook.**

**If a contribution drifts into directive language (‘God says you should’): redirect once. ‘Speak humbly: I heard, I sensed. The Discerner weighs.’**

**If a contribution drifts into the contributor’s own material (‘what came up for me was about my own life’): redirect. ‘Hold that for your standing pair; what did you hear FOR [Discerner’s name]?’**

**If a contribution names another cohort member by name as part of the calling (‘you should ask Sarah to come with you’): stop. ‘We are surfacing what we heard; specific personnel decisions are for the Discerner to weigh privately.’**

**If the Discerner becomes flooded mid-surfacing: Lead Companion pauses the round. Quiet check-in with the Discerner. Resume only if they are ready; otherwise integration without finishing the round is honest.**

**Block 7 — Discerner Integration + Possible Commissioning (8:15–8:23, 8 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“Thank you. [Discerner’s name], the body has surfaced what it heard. Take a breath. In your own time, briefly: what landed? What did you receive? What rings true; what is unfamiliar; what are you noticing as you weigh?”*

*“You do not have to integrate everything tonight. Most of what surfaced is for the next four to six weeks of integration. Tonight you name what you can; the rest you carry.”*

## Discerner’s integration (≈3 min)

**The Discerner names what landed. Specifically. ‘What I received as confirmation was \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘What surfaced that I had not named to myself yet was \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘What I am still weighing is \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘What did not ring true was \_\_\_\_\_.’**

**The Discerner does NOT have to render a verdict. Integration is partial. Some of what was surfaced will keep surfacing across the next 4–6 weeks.**

**If the Discerner is genuinely flooded, the integration can be brief: ‘What landed is too much to name tonight. I will integrate this week with my pair partner and the team.’ Honour.**

## Decision point — commissioning or integration-only (≈4 min)

**After the Discerner’s brief integration, the Lead Companion weighs aloud:**

*“I am noticing what surfaced converges around \_\_\_\_\_. Is the Discerner sensing the Spirit’s clear confirmation tonight, or is integration over the coming weeks the right next step?”*

**If the DISCERNER says clear confirmation: the Lead Companion invites the laying-on-of-hands. ‘We will lay hands and bless the sending. Cohort Companions — join me at the centre.’ The team gathers; lays hands on the Discerner; the Lead Companion (or the Discerner’s Cohort Companion) prays a specific blessing of sending. (3–4 min)**

**If the DISCERNER says integration over weeks: the Lead Companion names this honestly. ‘What was heard tonight is real and partial. [Discerner’s name] integrates with the team across the next weeks; the cohort holds them in prayer; what comes clearer in integration is what the Spirit is doing. There is no commissioning tonight; there may be one later. Both are honest outcomes of the Antioch architecture.’ (1–2 min)**

**If the cohort’s contributions converged BUT the Discerner is not ready: integration-only. The Discerner’s readiness, not the cohort’s convergence, is the discriminator. Do not pressure into commissioning.**

**Handout H10.1 — Acts 13 Antioch Reference**

*From IJH Vol 4. The architectural anchor for the apex of Going Deeper.*

*Now there were in the church at Antioch prophets and teachers, Barnabas, Simeon who was called Niger, Lucius of Cyrene, Manaen a member of the court of Herod the tetrarch, and Saul. While they were worshiping the Lord and fasting, the Holy Spirit said, ‘Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them.’ Then after fasting and praying they laid their hands on them and sent them off.*

*— Acts 13:1–3 (ESV)*

**The four-element architecture**

## WORSHIP + FAST — the body’s posture before listening. The team has lived this across the week.

**SPIRIT SPEAKS — specific guidance about specific people. The Spirit specifies; the body does not invent the calling.**

**BODY LAYS HANDS — the body confirms what the Spirit has spoken. Contingent on convergence; not automatic.**

**BODY SENDS — the discernment produces specific action. The Discerner walks; the cohort blesses the walking.**

**Three things to remember about Antioch**

## It was a small body. Five named people. Multi-ethnic, multi-class. Small bodies can do this work; large crowds cannot.

**The body had been formed before this moment. Antioch had been doing real work together for some time. Wk 10 of Going Deeper has nine prior weeks of formation underneath it.**

**The calling preceded the body’s discernment. Saul and Barnabas had been being called by the Spirit before the body ratified what the Spirit had been doing. The body recognizes; the Spirit calls.**

**Handout H10.2 — Discerner Calling Protocol**

**What you are being asked to do**
