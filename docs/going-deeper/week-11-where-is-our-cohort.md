## WEEK 11 — WHERE IS OUR COHORT (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Honestly assess where the cohort landed across Going Deeper on the five-level group taxonomy. Distinguish what was attempted, what was lived, what was missed, and what is still ahead. Receive the data without grading or pretending. Set up Wk 12’s re-commissioning on truthful foundations.

**Anchor scripture.** 1 Corinthians 13:11–12 (now we see in a mirror dimly); Philippians 1:6 (He who began a good work will bring it to completion); Hebrews 12:1–2 returning as the architectural anchor of the whole of Going Deeper.

**Connect focus.** Integration. Not Self / Others / God / Mission separately — all four read together as the cohort’s overall trajectory.

**Mode.** Cohort-split. Each cohort circle assesses itself first using the worksheet (35 min). Then merge for cross-cohort integration (25 min). The merge does the harder work of combining three honest cohort-level pictures into one cohort-level picture.

**Center.** Each cohort uses H11.1 and H11.2 to walk eleven weeks together — what was attempted, what was lived, where the cohort actually is on the five-level taxonomy. The merge surfaces commonalities and divergences across the three cohorts; the Lead Companion integrates the picture; the room receives without pretending.

**Between-session practice.** Each member writes a 1-page personal reflection: where I have moved, where I have not, what was the surprise, what is the question I am carrying into Going Out. Bring it to Wk 12’s re-commissioning.

**IJH source.** MSFIG (5-level group taxonomy as the assessment frame); Vol 1 Exp 1 (Word→Hearing→Faith chain as the test — has the chain operated in this cohort?); Hebrews 12:1–2 returning.

## WATCH FOR (Week 11 specific risks — read twice)

*Honest assessment of a formation cohort is rare and counter-cultural. Most cohorts default to celebration (‘we did so much; everyone grew’) or to despair (‘we did not get where we hoped’). Both are dishonest. The architecture tonight produces honest data wherever it lands; the team commits to receiving it.*

**Performance of growth. The dominant temptation. Members may report what they think the team wants to hear, or what feels socially acceptable, rather than what is actually true. Cohort Companion models honest self-assessment first; the cohort follows.**

**Performance of struggle. The opposite temptation. Members may overstate their lack of growth to seem appropriately humble. Same redirect; same structural correction.**

**Cohort-vs-individual confusion. Tonight the question is the COHORT’s level, not each individual member’s personal growth. Cohort Companion names the distinction explicitly.**

**The cohort hearing about itself from Wk 8 not honored. If Wk 8 produced a specific corporate hearing about us, tonight’s assessment must engage it. Did what we heard about ourselves prove true across Wks 9 and 10? If Wk 8 named the cohort as not-yet-Level-4, tonight names whether Wks 9–10 changed that.**

**The Asker integration not honored. Wk 10’s Asker is doing daily integration this week. If the cohort’s assessment ignores the Asker’s journey, the cohort is performing detachment. The Asker may speak briefly tonight in the merge if welcomed; Cohort Companion pre-checks with the Asker on Saturday.**

**Generational scapegoating. ‘Our cohort would have been at Level 4 if it weren’t for the [teens / parents].’ Stop. The cohort is one body; the assessment is one body’s assessment. Cohort Companion redirects firmly.**

**Members blaming or grading the team. Possible and sometimes warranted. Receive without defensiveness. The team’s pre-meet has rehearsed receiving feedback; the team takes notes; pastoral 1:1 with members who name specific team failures within the week.**

**Pre-convergence by the Lead Companion at the merge. The integration is honest data, not a synthesis the lead engineers. The lead reflects what each cohort named; resists the temptation to package it.**

**Members expecting Wk 12 to fix what Wk 11 reveals. Wk 12 is re-commissioning, not repair. If the cohort landed at Level 3 with Level 4 ahead, Wk 12 commissions the cohort at Level 3 with Level 4 ahead. The assessment does not predetermine Going Out; it grounds it.**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 11)

*Crisis risk is low. Most of what surfaces will be assessment material. The contingencies below are for the small set of acute possibilities.*

**If a cohort circle’s assessment surfaces a specific Cohort Companion failure or a team failure that members name with affect (‘I felt unsupported by [Companion] when I\_\_\_\_\_’). Receive without rushing or defending. Cohort Companion does not adjudicate in the room; pastoral 1:1 with the speaker within 48 hours; team-internal review at the post-Wk-11 debrief.**

**If a member discloses that the cohort’s formation produced a harm to them that has not been named (a relational rupture, a triggering experience, a way the architecture failed for their specific situation). Receive without rushing. Pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours; clinical referral as appropriate. Section 6 protocol if the harm crossed thresholds.**

**If the Asker from Wk 10 is in mid-integration crisis and Wk 11’s assessment surfaces material that adds load. Cohort Companion stays close to the Asker; Lead Companion follows up that night. The Asker’s integration takes precedence; pause Wk 11’s assessment briefly if needed.**

**If a member announces during the assessment that they are not continuing into Going Out (the Going Out series). Receive without alarm. The decision is theirs; the assessment may be part of their honest discernment. Pastoral 1:1 within the week.**

**If a teen-parent dynamic surfaces during cross-cohort merge. Cohort Companions handle outside the session.**

**Default. Section 6 of the Going Deeper Handbook. Pastoral / clinical backup confirmed by name and number for the night.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

From MSFIG of IJH: ‘Most groups never reach Level 4. Most that do reach it briefly and slip back. The honest assessment of where a cohort actually landed is the precondition for the next leg of the journey — cohorts that pretend to be at Level 5 when they are at Level 3 will collapse the moment the next stretch demands what Level 5 capacity supports.’

Going Deeper has run eleven weeks. Each week attempted specific work; each week landed somewhere on the spectrum between what was hoped and what was possible given the cohort’s actual capacity in that moment. Some weeks produced more than expected; some less; both happen in formation. The integration question tonight is not which weeks succeeded, but where the cohort itself is now — what capacity it has built, what is still emerging, what is still missing.

The five-level taxonomy is the working frame. Most cohorts begin Going Deeper at Level 2 or low Level 3, reach Level 3 reliably across the diagnostic and confessional blocks (Wks 2–5), attempt Level 4 in the God-deepened block (Wks 6–8), and engage Level 5 selectively for one Asker in Wk 10. The honest question tonight is what the cohort’s steady state is now — not the peak; the steady state. Where can the cohort operate at when it walks into Going Out?

Hebrews 12:1–2 returns as the anchor. Going Deeper walked the verse: witnesses (Wk 1, the cohort itself), weight (Wks 2–3), sin (Wk 5), endurance (the rhythm card across all weeks), looking to Jesus (Wks 6–8), the joy set before (Wks 9–10), finishing well (Wks 11–12). Tonight’s assessment is a check on whether the verse was lived or only spoken about. The data lands wherever it lands.

The team’s commitment in pre-meet is rigorous: receive what the cohort names without defensiveness, without pre-converging the merge, without engineering a more uplifting outcome than the data supports. Most pilots report Wk 11 as the hardest session of Going Deeper for the team — hearing honest assessment of one’s own facilitation requires the same architecture the team has been teaching the cohort. The team practices what it has preached.

## Script (the read)

*“Two passages tonight. Listen.”*

*When I was a child, I spoke like a child, I thought like a child, I reasoned like a child. When I became a man, I gave up childish ways. For now we see in a mirror dimly, but then face to face. Now I know in part; then I shall know fully, even as I have been fully known.*

*And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ.*

**Teaching points (use any format — do not read this verbatim)**

**•** Notice what Paul does in 1 Corinthians 13. He does not promise that we see clearly now. He says we see in a mirror dimly. The honest assessment of where we are includes the limit of our self-perception — we know in part. Tonight’s assessment is what the cohort can see now, dimly; not the final picture; the current picture, honestly named.

**•** Notice what Paul does in Philippians 1:6. He does not promise that the work is finished. He says he is sure that the One who began the work will bring it to completion. The completion is not now. Tonight’s assessment is mid-work data, not final-work data.

**•** These two together are the architecture of honest assessment in a Christian formation cohort. We see in part (so we don’t over-claim); we trust the One who completes (so we don’t despair). The data tonight is honest. The data is also incomplete. Both are true.

**Block 5 — Cohort Circles Assess Themselves (7:30–8:05, 35 min)**

Each cohort circle works in parallel. The structure is identical.

**Inside the cohort circle — Companion script**

## Opening (3 min)

*“Thirty-five minutes. We are assessing this cohort, not each individual’s growth.”*

*“The worksheet has six questions — H11.2. We work them in order. Some we will spend longer on; some shorter. The Cohort Companion captures key observations on the flip chart.”*

*“Two disciplines. One — specificity. ‘Our cohort grew in love’ is generic and unverifiable. ‘Our cohort showed up for [a specific moment in Wk 5 or Wk 7] in a way it could not have on Wk 1’ is honest and verifiable. Two — honesty over both flattery and condemnation. ‘We are at Level 4’ is most likely flattery; ‘we are still at Level 2’ is most likely condemnation; the truth is in the middle and is named specifically.”*

*“I’ll start the first question to model the architecture; you take over for the rest.”*

## The six questions (≈30 min total — vary as the cohort needs)

**Q1 — What was attempted. Walk the eleven weeks. Each week briefly: what did the cohort attempt? Specific. (5 min)**

**Q2 — What was lived. Of what was attempted, what did the cohort actually live? Specific. ‘Wk 5 confession was lived; the architecture held; we walked in restoration across the week.’ ‘Wk 8 corporate hearing was attempted but the cohort did not converge; we are honest about that.’ (8 min)**

**Q3 — What was missed. What was attempted that the cohort touched but did not live? What was not attempted that we should have? What did we not see across Going Deeper? (5 min)**

**Q4 — The Wk 8 hearing. If Wk 8 produced a specific hearing about us, did it prove true across Wks 9 and 10? If Wk 8 named the cohort as not-yet-Level-4, did the Mission block move us closer or confirm Level 3? (5 min)**

**Q5 — The five-level steady state. Where on the taxonomy is the cohort’s STEADY STATE right now? Not the peak. The steady state. Specific evidence. (5 min)**

**Q6 — What is ahead. What is the question or capacity the cohort needs to engage in Going Out (Going Out)? Specific. (2 min)**

**Cohort Companion: when to intervene**

**•** If the cohort is being uniformly positive (‘everything went well’) — push: ‘What did we touch but not live? Specific.’

**•** If the cohort is being uniformly negative (‘we didn’t really get there’) — push: ‘What did we live? Specific. Wk 5? Wk 7? A specific moment?’

**•** If the cohort’s assessment becomes about individual members rather than the body — redirect: ‘The question is the cohort’s level, not [member’s] growth.’

**•** If members blame the team or another cohort — receive without defending; capture the observation on the flip chart honestly; redirect: ‘And what about the cohort itself — not the team, not the other cohort.’

**•** If the cohort gets stuck on Q4 (the Wk 8 hearing) because the cohort is not sure what was heard — honest: ‘We are not sure what we heard at Wk 8. That itself is part of tonight’s data — the cohort either remembers or not.’

**•** If the cohort is afraid to name a specific level on Q5 — lead by example. ‘My honest assessment of where we are is Level [X]. Here is the specific evidence. What does the cohort see?’

**•** Watch the time. Q5 must happen; do not let earlier questions consume the time.

**•** By 7:55, the Cohort Companion finalizes the flip-chart picture so it can be brought to the merge.

**Differentiation by Cohort**

**Junior teens (12–14)**

## Adjustments

**Junior teens often assess concretely. ‘We were better at listening on Wk 7 than Wk 1.’ ‘We laughed more in our cohort circle by Wk 6.’ The Cohort Companion builds the assessment from the concrete observations the juniors bring.**

**The five-level taxonomy is harder for juniors to abstract. The Cohort Companion translates: ‘Were we mostly people in the same room (Level 1), people who shared information (Level 2), or people who did real work together (Level 3)?’ Most junior cohorts will land at Level 2 to low Level 3 honestly.**

**Watch for: junior teens who assess punitively (‘we were not as good as the older cohorts’). Re-frame: ‘Cohorts at different ages develop differently; we are assessing OUR cohort.’**

**Watch for: junior teens whose assessment is shaped by friendship dynamics (‘our cohort was great because we became friends’). Honour the friendship; clarify: ‘Friendship is part of what cohorts produce; the question is also what work we did together.’**

**Watch for: junior teens who name a specific Companion failure or team failure. Receive without defending; pastoral 1:1 if the issue is acute.**

**Senior teens (15–18)**

## Adjustments

**Senior teens often assess sharply. They see structures clearly and may critique with precision. Receive the precision; do not defend.**

**Handout H11.1 — Five-Level Taxonomy Reference**

*From IJH MSFIG. The same content as Wk 8’s H8.1, returning here for the assessment.*

**The five levels**

## LEVEL 1 — People in the same room. Co-presence without acknowledgment.

**LEVEL 2 — People who know each other and share information. Most small groups operate here.**

**LEVEL 3 — People who do real work together. Specific work surfaces in specific members; the room holds it. Wks 2–7 of Going Deeper operated here.**

**LEVEL 4 — A body that hears together. The group has agency; the Spirit speaks to it as a unit. Wk 8’s attempt.**

**LEVEL 5 — An apostolic team that is sent together. Acts 13:1–3. Wk 10’s attempt for one Asker.**

**Steady state vs. peak**

## Most cohorts have a peak moment that is one level above their steady state. Wk 5 confession or Wk 10 commissioning may have produced a moment of higher capacity.

**The peak is real; it is also not the steady state. The steady state is where the cohort can operate FROM in ordinary conditions — the next Tuesday after the peak; the difficult conversation between sessions; Going Out stretch.**

**Wk 11’s assessment is of the steady state. Naming the peak honestly is part of the assessment; pretending the peak is the steady state is the danger.**

**Specific evidence**

## How a cohort knows what its steady state is

**Level 3 evidence: members regularly name specific personal work in cohort circles; the cohort holds the work without rescuing or interpreting; pair work happens consistently between sessions.**

**Level 4 evidence: the cohort can hear something specific about itself, weigh it together, articulate what it heard, and act on it. This requires Level 3 first.**

**Level 5 evidence: the cohort can hear something specific about a member’s calling, confirm or correct it together, commission, and send. This requires Level 4 first.**
