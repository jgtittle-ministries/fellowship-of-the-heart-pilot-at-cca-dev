## WEEK 8 — WHAT THE GROUP HEARS ABOUT ITSELF (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Attempt Group Level 4 work — the cohort listening together for what the Spirit is saying about the cohort itself, not about individuals. Use the Revelation 2–3 frame (the Spirit addresses a body with name + commendation + critique + call + promise). Surface what each member heard FOR US; weigh the patterns; receive what coheres.

**Anchor scripture.** Ephesians 4:11–16 (the body building itself up); Revelation 2–3 selectively (the Spirit’s direct address to seven specific bodies with seven specific patterns); 1 Corinthians 14:29 (let the others weigh).

**Connect focus.** God (deepened) — apex of the God-deepened block. Wk 6 was individual hearing; Wk 7 was corporate hearing for one Bringer; Wk 8 is corporate hearing for the corporate.

**Mode.** Whole-room. No cohort split. The cohort sits as one circle with no centre chair tonight — the cohort itself is the subject of the hearing.

**Center.** Each participant listens in silence FOR THE COHORT for 12 minutes — not for themselves, not for individuals, for the body. Each member then briefly contributes what they heard ‘about us’. The Lead Companion reflects back the patterns — what is converging across multiple members — and the cohort weighs whether what was heard rings true.

**Between-session practice.** Each member journals what was heard about the cohort and what they themselves received from being part of what was heard. Standing-pair check-in this week explicitly: ‘What did the cohort hear about itself, and how does that land in me?’

**IJH source.** MSFIG (the 5-level group taxonomy — Level 4 is hearing-as-a-body, 75% confidence as a developmental ceiling for most cohorts); Vol 1 Exp 1 (Word→Hearing→Faith chain in corporate frame); Vol 2 Exp 9 (Community as Amplifier — the danger named here is most acute).

## WATCH FOR (Week 8 specific risks — read twice)

*Group Level 4 work is the formation step most cohorts never reach. The risks are not primarily clinical; they are diagnostic and pastoral. The session may produce something specific the cohort genuinely heard about itself; or it may produce honest data that the cohort is not yet at Level 4. Both outcomes are useful. The team holds the room either way without rushing the result.*

**Drift from ‘about us’ to ‘about me’. Members will instinctively surface what they heard for themselves. Redirect: ‘What did you hear about US? The Spirit’s word to the cohort, not the cohort’s word to you.’**

**Drift to ‘about him’ or ‘about her’. Members may hear something about a specific other member. Redirect: ‘We are not addressing individuals tonight. If something specific surfaced about a particular member, hold it for pastoral 1:1 outside the session. The hearing tonight is for the body.’**

**Projection dressed as hearing. ‘I think God is saying we need to be more \_\_\_\_\_.’ ‘We have a problem with \_\_\_\_\_.’ The Lead Companion listens for whether the contribution is the participant’s own concern theologized, or genuine corporate hearing. The weighing step is where the cohort sorts this together; do not pre-sort by intervening early.**

**Political or generational projection. A parent says ‘God is saying our young people need to\_\_\_\_\_.’ A senior teen says ‘God is saying our parents have to\_\_\_\_\_.’ These are almost always projection. The Lead Companion names the pattern at the integration block, gently, and invites the cohort to weigh whether the contribution actually came from listening or from prior conviction.**

**False prophecy by name. Any contribution that is directive, authoritative, or coercive (‘God says we MUST do X by Y’) is named as not-the-architecture. The Rev 2–3 frame helps: even when the Spirit critiques the churches, the call is conditional and the promise is for those who overcome — not coercive. Lead Companion gently models the architecture if it slips.**

**Group-level despair. Some cohorts hear something hard about themselves and the room sinks. Receive without rushing toward repair. Honest data is not despair; the Spirit’s critique in Rev 2–3 always precedes promise. Lead Companion holds the harder reflections without immediately consoling.**

**Group-level grandiosity. Some cohorts hear something that is mostly affirming and the room inflates. The Lead Companion weighs: ‘Is what we heard the Spirit’s real word to us, or what we wanted to hear?’ The 1 Cor 14:29 weighing is the discipline.**

**The participant who hears nothing for the cohort. Some members will not surface anything tonight — they were present, listened, and nothing for the cohort came clear. Affirm aggressively. ‘Hearing nothing is honest data. The body weighs together; not every member will hear every word.’ Listening without speaking is part of the discernment.**

**Confusion between Wk 7’s Bringer protocol and Wk 8. Members may instinctively contribute as if listening for an individual Bringer. There is no Bringer tonight. The cohort is the subject; the cohort is also the listener; the Lead Companion is the discernment guide.**

**Defaulting to Level 3 because Level 4 feels exposed. The cohort may, by the end, decide that what was heard is mostly each member’s individual hearing about themselves rather than corporate hearing about the body. That is honest. The team names that the cohort attempted Level 4 tonight and is at Level 3 with Level 4 ahead. The naming is part of the formation; do not contort the data to claim arrival.**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 8)

*Crisis risk is low to moderate. The risks are pastoral and discernment-related more than clinical. The contingencies below are for the small set of acute possibilities.*

**If a contribution names a specific cohort member negatively. Stop. The Lead Companion intervenes immediately: ‘We are listening for what the Spirit says about US as a body. Anything specific about an individual member belongs in pastoral 1:1, not the cohort.’ Receive without re-stating the content. Pastoral follow-up with both the contributor and the named member within 48 hours.**

**If a contribution names a coercive directive (‘We have to fire X / shun X / leave X’). Stop. ‘What was named is not the architecture of corporate hearing in the New Testament. We are not making decisions tonight based on contributions from this format; the cohort is hearing what the Spirit may be saying, and we weigh together. Authoritative claims like that move us out of the architecture.’ Pastoral follow-up with the contributor.**

**If a contribution opens material about active harm or abuse within the cohort. Section 6 protocol immediately. Mandatory-reporting law applies to whatever crosses thresholds. The cohort listening prayer is not the venue to disclose ongoing abuse; the Cohort Companion and Lead Companion engage outside the session.**

**If the cohort’s integrated picture is mostly negative and a member becomes flooded. Cohort Companion stays with the flooded member; Lead Companion continues holding the room. After: 24-hour contact; pastoral 1:1 within the week. The honest data does not hurt the body; the body’s rejection of the data is what hurts.**

**If the cohort cannot integrate — the contributions diverge widely with no convergence. Receive without forcing integration. ‘What we heard tonight does not yet cohere. That is honest data; the cohort is in formation. We will hold what was heard and revisit at Wk 11.’ Do not impose convergence the data does not support.**

**If a teen-parent dynamic surfaces in the contributions cross-cohort. Receive without naming the family. Pastoral support outside the session.**

**Default. Section 6 of the Going Deeper Handbook covers anything that crosses the safety threshold. Pastoral / clinical backup confirmed by name and number for the night.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

MSFIG of IJH names a five-level taxonomy for groups: Level 1 — people in the same room. Level 2 — people who know each other and share information. Level 3 — people who do real work together (the cohort circle level, where most of Going Deeper has operated). Level 4 — a body that hears together; the group has agency, and the Spirit speaks to it as a unit. Level 5 — an apostolic team that is sent together (the Acts 13 reality, which Wk 10 will engage).

From MSFIG: ‘Most groups never reach Level 4. Most that do reach it briefly and slip back. Level 4 is not a permanent state; it is a developmental capacity that becomes more frequent and easier to enter as a body matures. The diagnostic is whether the group can hear something specific, weigh it together, and act on it without dissolving into individual reactions.’ The 75% confidence rating reflects strong New Testament grounding (Acts 13:1–3, Acts 15, Revelation 2–3) plus consistent observation in formation communities, with the caveat that Level 4 is hard to test from outside and easy to confuse with Level 3 plus enthusiasm.

Tonight is the cohort’s explicit attempt at Level 4. The architecture is Revelation 2–3. In each of the seven letters, the Spirit addresses a specific body — the church at Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, Laodicea — with a specific structure: I know (the Spirit knows the body’s real condition); a specific commendation (what is true and good); a specific critique (where the body has drifted); a specific call (what needs to change); a specific promise (what overcoming looks like). Tonight’s cohort listening uses this frame as a working format — not because we expect a chapter of Revelation to materialize, but because the structure of the Spirit’s address to a body has been given to us, and the cohort can listen for an analogue.

Two outcomes are possible tonight. One — the cohort hears something specific that coheres across multiple contributions, and the integration produces a recognizable analogue to a Rev 2–3 letter (with parts of one or more of the five elements). Two — the cohort hears partially, the contributions do not cohere into a clear pattern, and the integrated picture is honest data that the cohort is at Level 3 with Level 4 ahead. Both outcomes are useful. The team commits in advance not to engineer outcome one or to despair at outcome two.

Wk 6 was individual hearing; Wk 7 was corporate hearing for one Bringer; Wk 8 is corporate hearing for the corporate. The arc completes the God-deepened block. Wks 9, 10, 11, 12 then engage what the cohort heard about itself — the Mission block tests it (shadow mission, Antioch calling) and the Integration block honest-assesses it (where is our cohort, sending).

**Pre-Work for the Companion Team (this week)**

**Personal pre-work**

Each Companion does three things this week.

First — read Revelation 2–3 in full, slowly, across two sittings. Notice the structure of the Spirit’s address to each church. Notice that no two letters have the same content; each body is addressed specifically, with what was specifically true of that body. Notice also that no body got only commendation or only critique; the structure holds for all seven.

Second — sit for 30 minutes with this question: ‘If the Spirit dictated a Revelation 2–3-style letter to our cohort tonight, what might He say?’ Listen carefully. Write what comes — even fragments, even uncertain hearings. The point is not to arrive at the answer; the point is to enter the room having already listened. The Lead Companion who has not listened privately first will struggle to hold the room’s listening tonight.

Third — do not bring what you wrote to the team meeting. The danger is that the team produces a pre-cooked answer that the room then discovers. Each Companion listens privately; each Companion brings only their own hearing posture, not their content; the cohort listens fresh.

**Block 3 — Group Taxonomy and Rev 2–3 (Teaching) (7:13–7:30, 17 min)**

## Script (the read)

*“Tonight’s anchor scripture is the address to one of the seven churches. Listen.”*

*(Read Revelation 2:1–7 — the letter to Ephesus — from the physical Bible, slowly. Pick a different letter if your team’s pre-meet discerned one is more apt for this cohort.)*

*To the angel of the church in Ephesus write... I know your works, your toil and your patient endurance, and how you cannot bear with those who are evil... But I have this against you, that you have abandoned the love you had at first. Remember therefore from where you have fallen; repent, and do the works you did at first... He who has an ear, let him hear what the Spirit says to the churches.*

*— Revelation 2:1–7 (ESV, abbreviated)*

**Teaching points (use any format — do not read this verbatim)**

**•** Notice what the Spirit does in Rev 2–3. He addresses bodies, not individuals. The letter is to the church at Ephesus — not to the pastor, not to a member, to the body. Each of the seven letters is addressed to a body. The pattern is a New Testament given: the Spirit speaks to bodies as bodies.

**•** The structure of each letter is consistent. ‘I know’ — the Spirit knows the body’s real condition. Specific commendation — what is true and good. Specific critique — where the body has drifted. Specific call — what needs to change. Specific promise — what overcoming looks like. Five elements. No two letters have identical content; each body is addressed for what is specifically true of it.

**•** This is the architecture for what we are attempting tonight. We do not expect a chapter of Revelation to materialize in the room. We expect that the Spirit, who has spoken to bodies in the New Testament with this structure, may speak to our cohort tonight in something that resembles parts of this structure. Maybe the I-know clause comes clear; maybe a commendation surfaces; maybe a critique. Whatever surfaces, we listen, contribute, weigh.

## The five-level group taxonomy (MSFIG)

**LEVEL 1 — People in the same room. Co-presence without acknowledgment.**

**LEVEL 2 — People who know each other and share information. Most small groups operate here.**

**LEVEL 3 — People who do real work together. The cohort circle level. Specific work surfaces in specific members; the room holds it. Wks 2–7 of Going Deeper have operated here.**

**LEVEL 4 — A body that hears together. The group has agency; the Spirit speaks to it as a unit. Tonight’s attempt.**

**LEVEL 5 — An apostolic team that is sent together. Acts 13:1–3. Wk 10 will engage this directly.**

*“Most groups never reach Level 4. Most that reach it do so briefly and slip back. The honest truth tonight is that we may attempt Level 4 and end the session at Level 3. That outcome is honest data, not failure. We will name it honestly at the integration block if that is what the data shows. But we attempt; that is the discipline.”*

**How the listening tonight will run**

## Twelve minutes of silent listening. Each member listens FOR the cohort — not for themselves. The disciplines: do not chase your own material; do not listen for what you already think the cohort needs; listen with hands open.

**Around-the-circle surfacing of what each heard ABOUT US. Brief; 30–60 seconds per member; pass anytime. The Lead Companion captures contributions in a notebook.**

**Pattern integration. The Lead Companion reflects what is converging across multiple members — if anything is. The cohort weighs together: does this ring true? Is it the Spirit’s word to us, or is it what we wanted to hear? 1 Corinthians 14:29.**

**If patterns converge, we name what we heard, in our own words, and decide what to do with it (most often: hold; revisit at Wk 11). If patterns do not converge, we name that honestly and move on.**

**Block 4 — Silent Listening for the Cohort (7:30–7:42, 12 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“Twelve minutes of silence. Listen FOR the cohort. Not for yourself. Not for individual members. For US.”*

*“The disciplines: hands open; no agenda; if your own material surfaces, set it aside and return to listening for the body. If you hear something specific, mark it briefly on H8.3 — a phrase, a word, a sense, even a fragment. Specificity helps the contribution later.”*

*“If nothing surfaces, that is honest. Listening without speaking is part of the body’s discernment.”*

*“Twelve minutes. Begin.”*

## During the silence (12 min)

**Hold the FULL twelve minutes. Do not shorten because the room feels heavy. The silence is part of what produces real hearing.**

**The Lead Companion listens too — not performatively, but actually. The team’s hearing is part of the body’s hearing.**

**Watch for any participant who is visibly distressed during the silence. If a participant raises a hand, a Cohort Companion goes to them quietly. If a participant leaves the circle, a Cohort Companion follows discreetly.**

**At about 8 min, gentle reminder if needed: ‘Halfway. Stay with listening for the body.’ Most cohorts do not need this; some do.**

**At 12 min, quiet bell or named close: ‘Time. Take a breath together.’**

**Block 5 — Surfacing What Each Heard About Us (7:42–8:05, 23 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“Welcome back. Around the circle. ONE thing each of you heard ABOUT US during the silence — not about yourself, not about individual members, about the cohort. Brief; 30 to 60 seconds. Pass anytime.”*

*“If you heard nothing for the cohort, say so. ‘I listened; nothing specific surfaced for the body.’ That is honest. Listening without speaking is part of the discernment.”*

*“Speak humbly. ‘I heard,’ ‘I sensed,’ ‘what came to me was —’. Not ‘God says we have to.’ The architecture of the contributions is humble offerings; the body weighs.”*

*“I will capture each contribution in my notebook. I am not editing or interpreting; I am holding what each of you offers so we can weigh together. I will start; one of the Cohort Companions will go second to model the architecture.”*

## How surfacing runs (≈21 min for content)

**Lead Companion goes first. ONE specific thing they heard about the cohort, briefly. Models the language: ‘What came to me was that our cohort \_\_\_\_\_’.**

**One Cohort Companion goes second. Same architecture. Models that the team listens too.**

**Around the circle. Each member 30–60 seconds. Pass anytime. With 24 people that is 12–20 minutes; the contribution length adjusts to fit.**

**Lead Companion captures every contribution in a notebook — short phrases. The integration block depends on this record.**

**If a contribution drifts to ‘about me’: gently redirect once. ‘Hold what you heard for yourself; bring it to your standing pair this week. What did you hear about US?’**

**If a contribution names an individual: stop immediately. ‘We are listening for what the Spirit says about US. Anything specific about an individual member belongs in pastoral 1:1, not the cohort.’ Do not re-state the content.**

**If a contribution is coercive (‘God says we MUST’): name the architecture. ‘The Rev 2–3 letters even when they are critique are conditional; the call is to repent, the promise is to those who overcome. Coercive directives move us out of the architecture. Restate humbly or pass.’**

**Block 6 — Pattern Integration + Cohort Weighing (8:05–8:20, 15 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (3 min)

*“Thank you. We have many contributions in front of us. The work now is integration — we look together for what is converging across multiple members. The Spirit’s word to a body is most often heard when the same thing surfaces independently in many members. The convergence is the discernment.”*

*“I am going to read back, briefly, a few patterns I noticed in what you contributed. Some are clear; some are tentative. After I read them back, the cohort weighs — does this ring true to us as the Spirit’s word to our body?”*

*(Lead Companion names 2–4 patterns from the contributions, briefly.)*

*“Pattern 1 — several of you named \_\_\_\_\_. Pattern 2 — several of you named \_\_\_\_\_. Pattern 3 — several of you named \_\_\_\_\_. (Specific. Drawn from actual contributions. The Lead Companion does not invent patterns; reflects what is in the record.)”*

## Cohort weighing (8 min)

*“The weighing question. For each pattern: does this ring true to us? Is it the Spirit’s word to our body, or is it what we wanted to hear, or is it what some of us already thought before the silence?”*

*“Three to five voluntary contributions. Each one brief: ‘Pattern 1 rings true to me; here is why.’ Or: ‘I am not sure about pattern 2; I notice it landed differently across the cohort.’ The weighing is the discipline.”*

*(Receive 3–5 contributions. Lead Companion listens without commentary.)*

## Naming what was heard (4 min)

*“Two possibilities here. One — the cohort can articulate, in one or two sentences, what we heard about us tonight. Two — the cohort cannot fully articulate yet, and we hold what was heard in pieces, with intention to revisit.”*

*(If convergence emerged:) “What I am hearing the cohort name is roughly: \_\_\_\_\_. Does that capture it? (Brief refinement from the cohort. The Lead Companion does not impose; the cohort articulates.)”*

*(If convergence did not emerge:) “What I am hearing is that the contributions tonight did not converge into a clear word. That is honest data. We attempted Level 4; we are at Level 3 with Level 4 ahead. We hold the contributions in our journals and revisit at Wk 11. The honesty is part of the formation.”*

*“Either way — a Rev 2–3-style address to a body always points forward to overcoming. Whatever we heard tonight, we hold it gently, weigh it across the week, and bring it into the rest of Going Deeper.”*

**Senior teens (15–18)**

## Adjustments

**Senior teens often hear systemic patterns — what the cohort is doing well, where it is performing, where it is shrinking from harder work. The Lead Companion weights senior contributions equally, especially when they critique the cohort.**

**Watch for: the senior whose contribution is a generational projection (‘God is saying our parents need to\_\_\_\_\_’). Same redirect as in the WATCH FOR section: this is almost always projection. Receive without commentary; reflect to the cohort during integration without naming the senior.**

**Watch for: the senior who hears something about the cohort’s relationship to Christ that is theologically sharp (‘we are good at the work and not as good at the worship’). Honour. Sharpness from a senior teen often lands more than from an adult; let the cohort weigh it.**

**Watch for: the senior who passes because they think their hearing might offend. Cohort Companion briefly checks: ‘Anything for the body you held back?’ No pressure; the offer is the support.**

**Watch for: the senior whose hearing crosses thresholds (a sense that the cohort is doing harm, a sense of withdrawal). Pastoral 1:1 within 48 hours; the senior’s discernment may be real.**

**Parents**

## Adjustments

**Parents often hear the cohort’s long-term arc — what has shifted across Getting Started-and-fall journey, what has stayed stuck, what is just beginning to form. The Lead Companion gives parental contributions weight without imposing them on the body’s weighing.**

**Watch for: the parent whose contribution is the same generational projection in reverse (‘God is saying our young people have to \_\_\_\_\_’). Same redirect.**

**Watch for: the parent who hears something specific about the cohort’s relationship to the broader church or community. Honour; the cohort is not isolated, and the Spirit may speak about how the body relates to bodies it is part of.**

**Watch for: the parent who hears something they sense their own teen-in-the-cohort needs to receive. The hearing is for the body; the parent does not appropriate it as a parental message. Pastoral 1:1 if the dynamic is acute.**

**Watch for: the parent whose hearing is about the team itself — the Companions. Receive humbly. The team is part of the cohort; what is heard about US includes us. The team commits in advance not to deflect this category of hearing.**

**Watch for: the parent whose hearing produces an action item (‘we need to start doing X’). The cohort tonight is not a decision-making body; the hearing is held, weighed, and revisited at Wk 11. The parent’s impulse to act is normal; the discipline is to hold.**

**Handout H8.1 — Group Level Taxonomy + Architecture**

*From IJH MSFIG. Carry this card; the taxonomy is the working frame for Wk 11’s honest cohort assessment.*

**The five levels of group formation**

## LEVEL 1 — People in the same room. Co-presence without acknowledgment.

**LEVEL 2 — People who know each other and share information. Most small groups operate here.**

**LEVEL 3 — People who do real work together. The cohort circle level. Specific work surfaces in specific members; the room holds it. Wks 2–7 of Going Deeper have operated here.**

**LEVEL 4 — A body that hears together. The group has agency; the Spirit speaks to it as a unit. Wk 8’s attempt.**

**LEVEL 5 — An apostolic team that is sent together. Acts 13:1–3. Wk 10 will engage this directly.**

**What Level 4 actually looks like**

## The body listens together for the Spirit’s word about the body itself.

**Multiple members hear something specific that converges across contributions — the same theme, image, call, commendation, or critique surfaces in independent hearings.**

**The body weighs the convergence together (1 Cor 14:29). The discernment is corporate, not individual.**

**The body articulates, in its own words, what it heard.**

**The body holds what was heard and decides together what to do with it.**

**Why Level 4 is rare**

## Level 4 requires Level 3 first. A body cannot hear together if it is not yet doing real work together. Going Deeper has built Level 3 across Wks 2–7; tonight is the first Level 4 attempt.

**Level 4 requires honest data. Bodies that perform unity rather than living it cannot weigh what they heard. The cohort’s honesty across Going Deeper is the precondition for tonight’s attempt being real.**

**Level 4 is not permanent. Even bodies that reach Level 4 do not stay there constantly; they enter it for specific occasions and return to Level 3. The Acts 13 paradigm names a specific moment when a body listened and acted; the same body operated at Level 3 most of the time.**

**Level 4 is not a status to claim. The cohort’s honest assessment at Wk 11 may be that the cohort attempted Level 4 at Wk 8 and is at Level 3 with Level 4 ahead. That is honest data.**

**Handout H8.2 — Revelation 2–3 Case Study**

*The Spirit’s address to seven specific bodies in seven specific patterns. The architecture for what we attempted tonight.*

**The five elements**

## ‘I KNOW’ — The Spirit knows the body’s real condition. Not what the body thinks of itself; what is actually true.

**SPECIFIC COMMENDATION — What is true and good in the body. Not generic. Specific.**

**SPECIFIC CRITIQUE — Where the body has drifted. Not condemnation; correction. Specific.**

**SPECIFIC CALL — What the body is called to do in response. Conditional, not coercive.**

**SPECIFIC PROMISE — What overcoming looks like for the body. Forward-pointing.**

**The seven addresses**

Each of the seven bodies in Rev 2–3 received a different address. No two are identical; each body is addressed for what is specifically true of it. The diversity is part of the architecture — the Spirit does not say the same thing to every body.

## Patterns across the seven

**Ephesus (2:1–7) — commended for endurance and discernment; critiqued for abandoning first love; called to repent and return.**

**Smyrna (2:8–11) — commended in suffering; no critique recorded; called to faithfulness; promised the crown of life.**

**Pergamum (2:12–17) — commended for holding fast; critiqued for tolerating false teaching; called to repent.**

**Thyatira (2:18–29) — commended for love and faith; critiqued for tolerating Jezebel; called to hold fast.**

**Sardis (3:1–6) — little to commend; named as having a reputation of being alive while dead; called to wake up.**

**Philadelphia (3:7–13) — commended for keeping the word; no critique recorded; promised vindication.**

**Laodicea (3:14–22) — little to commend; critiqued for lukewarmness and self-deception; called to repent and let Christ in.**

**How the case study informs tonight**

## Whatever the cohort heard about itself tonight may resemble parts of one or more letters — maybe an I-know clause came clear; maybe a commendation surfaced; maybe a critique. We do not need all five elements; even one or two specific clauses, if they cohere across multiple members’ hearing, is meaningful.

**The Spirit’s critiques in Rev 2–3 are conditional, not coercive. ‘Repent’ is invitation, not threat-disguised-as-command. The promises are for those who overcome. The architecture is invitation-with-stakes, not coercion.**

**No body in Rev 2–3 is final. Each is addressed in its current condition; each is invited to overcome. The cohort’s honest assessment of what was heard tonight is data for the next leg of the journey, not a verdict.**
