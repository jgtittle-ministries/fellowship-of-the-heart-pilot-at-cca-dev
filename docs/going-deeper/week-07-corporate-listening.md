## WEEK 7 — CORPORATE LISTENING PRAYER (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Practice corporate listening prayer for ONE pre-briefed cohort member with a real present question. The room listens together for 20 minutes; each member speaks back briefly what they heard FOR the Bringer; the Bringer receives, weighs, and names what landed.

**Anchor scripture.** 1 Corinthians 14:29–33 (let two or three prophets speak and the others weigh; God is not a God of confusion); James 1:5 (if any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask God); Acts 13:1–3 read briefly as foreshadowing of Wk 10.

**Connect focus.** God (deepened). Second session of the God-deepened block. Wk 6’s individual extended hearing is the foundation; tonight is the corporate version.

**Mode.** Whole-room. No cohort split. The whole cohort sits as one circle with the Bringer’s chair in the centre. ONE Bringer pre-briefed Saturday. ONE real question. The cohort listens together; speaks back; the Bringer weighs.

**Center.** The Bringer’s real present question. Not theoretical, not extracted from past. A question alive in their life this season. The cohort’s job is to listen FOR the Bringer, not for themselves; speak what they heard humbly (‘I heard,’ ‘I sensed,’ ‘what came to me was’); the Bringer’s job is to receive with weight, not to be obligated to act on anything.

**Between-session practice.** Each cohort member journals what came to them for the Bringer that they did NOT speak aloud. The Bringer journals across the week the daily noticing of what landed. Standing pair carries the work; cohort-wide silent prayer for the Bringer’s situation continues across the week.

**IJH source.** Vol 1 Exp 1 (Word→Hearing→Faith chain in corporate context); Vol 1 Exp 4 (PROAPT extended to corporate listening); Vol 2 Exp 9 (Community as Amplifier — the Law and the Danger, 80% confidence) — the discernment requirement in 1 Cor 14:29 IS the Danger named in IJH.

## WATCH FOR (Week 7 specific risks — read twice)

*This session has more risk than co-processing demonstrations (Wk 4) and confession (Wk 5). The risk is not primarily clinical — it is spiritual. Misused, corporate listening prayer is the mechanism of spiritual manipulation, false prophecy, and cohort coercion. Used well with discernment built in, it is one of the most formational practices in Christian community.*

**Listeners speaking authoritatively. ‘God told me you should \_\_\_\_\_.’ The Lead Companion stops this immediately and reframes: ‘Speak in I-heard or I-sensed language. The Bringer weighs what is from God; we do not declare it FOR them.’**

**Listeners projecting their own material onto the Bringer. ‘What I heard for you is exactly what I am working on this season.’ Possible coincidence; more often projection. The Cohort Companion notices; the Bringer weighs.**

**The Bringer feeling obligated to act on everything spoken. The architecture explicitly grants the Bringer permission to set aside any of what was said. The framing in Block 8 names this; the H7.3 Bringer Reflection sheet names it again.**

**Pile-on confirmation. Three listeners say similar things; a fourth feels obligated to confirm even if their honest hearing was different. The Cohort Companion gently invites differing voices: ‘Was anyone’s hearing different from what has been spoken? Different is not wrong.’**

**The Bringer’s question is too big to be held in one session. ‘Should I divorce my husband?’ ‘Am I being called to leave my church?’ The pre-brief on Saturday should have screened questions of this magnitude; if one slipped through, the Lead Companion gently re-frames the question to a sub-question that can be honestly held tonight, with the larger question routed to pastoral discernment outside the session.**

**The Bringer disclosing material that exceeds what corporate prayer can hold (active suicidality, current abuse, mandatory-reporting territory). Section 6 protocol activates; the listening prayer pivots to crisis support; pastoral / clinical backup engaged.**

**Listeners using ‘what I heard’ to deliver feedback they wanted to give anyway. ‘God told me you should stop being so judgmental of your sister.’ The Lead Companion catches this: ‘That sounds like advice you are giving in God’s name. Speak only what came to you while you were listening, not what you wanted to say to [Bringer].’**

**Junior teens watching parents speak prophetically into other parents (or vice versa). Cross-cohort dynamics multiply. The team is briefed on which families are present and which speakings could land sideways; Cohort Companions watch their cohort members in real time.**

**The Bringer freezing in the centre chair. Corporate attention is heavy. The Lead Companion sits adjacent; is available; offers brief breaks if the Bringer signals they want one. The Bringer can choose not to weigh aloud at the end — they can name ‘I will weigh privately and report back to my standing pair’ instead.**

**The cohort applauding or affirming heavily after the speaking-back round. The applause instinct is real; the Lead Companion shuts it down at the start: ‘We do not applaud. Receiving in silence is the discipline.’**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 7)

*Crisis risk is moderate-to-high. Two-call protocol active. The Bringer’s pre-brief on Saturday is the primary risk-reduction; the rest of the contingencies handle live-session edge cases.*

**If the Bringer’s presented question reveals material that crosses thresholds (suicidality, current abuse, active intent to harm). Stop the listening prayer immediately. Lead Companion engages with the Bringer alongside one Cohort Companion; the room is dismissed briefly to a side room; pastoral / clinical backup engaged within 15 minutes. Section 6 protocol.**

**If a listener’s speaking-back delivers something the Bringer experiences as accusatory or shaming. The Lead Companion interrupts gently: ‘I want to pause here. [Bringer], you do not have to receive this; we will weigh together what was said. [Listener], please clarify the tone of what you heard.’ The room learns; the Bringer is not left holding the accusation.**

**If a listener’s speaking-back exposes something about the Bringer the cohort did not previously know. ‘I sensed something about your relationship with your father.’ Receive carefully; the Bringer chooses to engage or not engage in the moment; the listener does not press; pastoral 1:1 within the week if welcomed.**

**If the Bringer becomes flooded mid-session. Pause. The Lead Companion names: ‘We are going to take a 5-minute break. [Bringer], step out with [Cohort Companion]; the rest of us will sit in silence until you return.’ The Bringer can return or can name they cannot finish; if not, the speaking-back round is paused until the next Tuesday or rescheduled in pastoral 1:1.**

**If a listener cannot stop themselves from giving advice (despite the protocol). The Lead Companion quietly waves them off: ‘Thank you. Hold the rest in your standing-pair conversation this week.’ The room does not relitigate the moment; the listener is briefed afterward.**

**If multiple listeners speak the same theme strongly and the Lead Companion senses conviction (rare but real). The Lead Companion names what is happening without claiming authority: ‘Several of us have heard a similar theme. The convergence is data; [Bringer] weighs it; pastoral conversation may follow.’ No declaration that the convergence is from God.**

**Default. Section 6 of the Going Deeper Handbook. Pastoral / clinical backup confirmed by name and number. Lead Companion carries phone access in the room.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

Wk 6 extended PROAPT to longer passages and centred the Tell step — individual hearing made habitual. Wk 7 takes the next step: the cohort, which has been a parallel-work instrument since Wk 4 and a hearing instrument individually since Wk 6, becomes a CORPORATE hearing instrument tonight. The transition is structurally significant. From Wk 7 forward, what the cohort hears together carries weight that what individuals hear alone does not.

The scriptural grounding is 1 Cor 14:29–33. Paul allows two or three prophets to speak in the assembly; the others weigh what is said; God is not a God of confusion. Two structural elements jump out: corporate hearing is permitted and even encouraged; corporate hearing requires DISCERNMENT — the others weigh. Without discernment, prophecy becomes assertion; with discernment, prophecy becomes formation. Wk 7’s architecture builds the discernment in: every speaker uses I-heard or I-sensed framing, the Bringer weighs explicitly, the cohort is responsible for noticing convergence and divergence honestly.

Vol 2 Exp 9 of IJH names community as the amplifier of formation — and warns that the same amplification works in both directions. Tonight is where the warning bites. A cohort that can hear FOR a member is also a cohort that can pressure, manipulate, and falsely declare. The architecture is the discipline that makes the practice safe across years; the team’s vigilance in real time is the discipline that makes it safe tonight.

This session also previews Wk 8 (the cohort hearing about ITSELF) and Wk 10 (Acts 13 calling discernment for ONE member’s vocational call). Both Wk 8 and Wk 10 depend on the corporate-listening architecture being in place from tonight. If Wk 7 doesn’t install the discernment frame well, Wk 8 cannot do what Wk 8 needs to do.

**The Bringer pre-brief**

The Bringer is identified by Saturday before Wk 7. The Lead Companion and one Cohort Companion hold a 45-minute pre-brief with the Bringer, ideally in person.

## Pre-brief content

**ONE — The question must be real and present. Alive in the Bringer’s life this season. Not theoretical (‘where do I find God in suffering generally’); not extracted from past (‘what should I have done in 2018’); not a marriage or major-vocation rupture in active heat (those are pastoral 1:1 territory).**

**TWO — Examples that work: a vocational discernment with a near-term decision (‘do I pursue the master’s program or stay in current role’); a relational discernment that has been simmering (‘how do I hold my friendship with X across this season’); a sense-of-calling clarification (‘what is the next chapter of my mothering as kids enter independence’); a season-of-life question (‘what is the Spirit shaping in me through this stretch of waiting’).**

**THREE — The Bringer agrees to receive what the cohort speaks. They do not argue with listeners; they do not interpret listeners’ hearing in the moment. They listen, take notes if helpful, and weigh at the end.**

**FOUR — The Bringer agrees that they have explicit permission to set aside any of what is said. The cohort’s hearing is data, not directive. Discernment is theirs.**

**FIVE — The Bringer prepares a 4-minute presentation of the question. The pre-brief includes a rehearsal: the Bringer presents to the Lead Companion and Cohort Companion; they offer one or two clarifying refinements; the Bringer rewrites if needed.**

**SIX — The Bringer’s standing pair is informed by Sunday that their pair partner is the Wk 7 Bringer. The pair partner sits adjacent during the session; receives the Bringer’s notes afterward; walks the weighing with them in the days that follow.**

**Connect focus**

God, deepened. The corporate listening practice IS the deepened God Connect. Self is implicated only as the Bringer’s question; Others is the witnessing cohort; Mission is foreshadowed (Wks 9 and 10 will use the cohort-hearing skill on calling questions). Tonight, God is the agent and the speaker; the cohort is the instrument; the Bringer is the receiver.

**Block 3 — 1 Corinthians 14 and Corporate Listening (7:13–7:30, 17 min)**

## Script (the read)

*“Tonight’s scripture is 1 Corinthians 14, with one companion. Listen.”*

*Let two or three prophets speak, and let the others weigh what is said. If a revelation is made to another sitting there, let the first be silent... For God is not a God of confusion but of peace.*

*— 1 Corinthians 14:29–30, 33 (ESV, abbreviated)*

*If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask God, who gives generously to all without reproach, and it will be given him.*

*— James 1:5 (ESV)*

**Teaching points (use any format — do not read this verbatim)**

**•** Paul allows corporate prophecy in the assembly. Two or three speak; the others weigh. Notice the second clause as much as the first. Corporate hearing without weighing is assertion. Corporate hearing with weighing is formation.

**•** ‘Let the others weigh’ — the Greek is \*diakrinetōsan\*, the verb of discernment. The same verb shows up in 1 Cor 12:10 (‘the discerning of spirits’) and Hebrews 5:14 (‘powers of discernment trained by constant practice to distinguish good from evil’). Discernment is a practiced skill, not a feeling. The room exercises it together.

**•** ‘God is not a God of confusion but of peace.’ The marker of authentic corporate hearing is peace at the end — not unanimity, not certainty, but settled peace. Confusion at the end is data; the room weighs whether the confusion is in the speakers, in the Bringer’s receiving, or in the prematurity of the question being held tonight.

**•** James 1:5 names the asking. The Bringer is asking. The cohort is asking on the Bringer’s behalf. God gives generously without reproach; the question is whether we have the discipline to listen rather than impose our own answers.

## The architecture of tonight

**ONE Bringer. ONE real present question. The Bringer has been pre-briefed and has rehearsed.**

**20 minutes silent corporate listening. The room prays for the Bringer’s situation by name and listens. NOT ‘what would I say’; ‘what is the Spirit putting on my heart for them.’**

**20 minutes speaking-back round. Each cohort member speaks briefly — about 30 seconds — what they heard FOR the Bringer. Framing language: ‘I heard,’ ‘I sensed,’ ‘what came to me was.’ NEVER ‘God told me you should.’ Pass if nothing specific came.**

**The Bringer receives in silence. Notes if helpful. Does not respond, argue, interpret, or thank during the round.**

**8 minutes for the Bringer to weigh. 5 minutes silent; 3 minutes to name briefly what landed and what is set aside. The Bringer has explicit permission to set aside any of what was said.**

**The Listener Protocol — three rules**

## RULE 1 — Listen FOR the Bringer, not for yourself. The Spirit may put your own material in your awareness during the silent listen; that is parallel work, not what you speak in the round. Speak only what came to you FOR the Bringer.

**RULE 2 — Frame in I-heard / I-sensed language. Never ‘God told me you should.’ Never ‘you must.’ The Bringer weighs; you do not declare.**

**RULE 3 — Pass if nothing specific came. Silence is honest. Filling silence with affirmation (‘I just want to say I love [Bringer] and I think God is doing great things’) is not corporate listening; it is sentiment. Pass.**

**Block 4 — Bringer Presents the Question (7:30–7:35, 5 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“[Bringer], come to the centre.”*

*(Bringer takes the centre chair. Lead Companion sits adjacent right; standing pair partner sits adjacent left.)*

*“[Bringer] has agreed to bring a real present question to the room tonight. They will speak for about 4 minutes; we will not interrupt; afterward I will briefly confirm understanding so the room is clear about what we are listening for. Then we move to silent corporate listening for 20 minutes.”*

*“[Bringer], take a breath. The room is with you. Speak when you are ready.”*

## How the Bringer’s presentation runs (≈4 min)

**Bringer presents the question. Specific. Present. Real. The pre-brief on Saturday refined the framing; tonight the Bringer delivers what was rehearsed.**

**Examples of well-formed Bringer questions:**

*— ‘I am in vocational discernment between [option A] and [option B] with a decision required by [date]. What is the Spirit shaping in me about which path?’*

*— ‘My friendship with [name] has been thinning across this season. I do not know whether to invest more, hold steady, or release. What is the Spirit saying about how I hold this?’*

*— ‘As my children move into the next stage, I sense my mothering / fathering needs to shift but do not know into what. What is the Spirit shaping me into?’*

**Lead Companion listens. No interruption. Notes the framing for the room’s clarity at the end.**

## Lead Companion script — confirmation (60 sec)

*“Thank you, [Bringer]. To make sure the room is clear about what we are listening for: the question is \_\_\_\_\_. (Repeat back the core of the question in one sentence.) Is that the question we should hold tonight?”*

*(Bringer confirms or briefly clarifies.)*

*“Good. Cohort, we listen for [Bringer] for the next 20 minutes. Pray for them by name; ask the Spirit to put on your heart what He has for them; sit with what surfaces. Notes if helpful; H7.2 has the Listener Protocol if you need to refer.”*

**Block 5 — Silent Corporate Listening (7:35–7:55, 20 min)**

## How the silent listen works

**Twenty minutes. Silence. The cohort prays for the Bringer by name and listens for what the Spirit puts on their heart FOR the Bringer.**

**Listeners can have their eyes open or closed. Some pray silently; some sit with the question; some journal what comes; some PROAPT a relevant passage briefly. There is no single right posture for the listening.**

**The Bringer sits in the centre chair. They may journal, sit silently with their eyes closed, breathe, or pray. The Lead Companion and the standing pair partner sit adjacent in supportive silence.**

**If a listener becomes flooded or distressed during the listen, they raise one hand; a Cohort Companion quietly comes to them; they can leave the circle. The listen continues without disruption to the centre.**

**The Lead Companion watches the time. At 18 min, gentle whisper to nearest Cohort Companion: ‘Two minutes.’ At 20 min, Lead Companion names the close.**

*Watch for: a listener who appears distressed or has been crying for an extended portion of the listen. Cohort Companion notes; offline check-in at the close.*

*Watch for: a listener who is taking copious notes for an extended portion. Possible: real hearing being captured. Possible: anxiety dressed as hearing. The speaking-back round will surface which.*

*Watch for: the Bringer who is visibly flooded mid-listen. The Lead Companion can gently offer a 30-second reset — ‘Take a breath, [Bringer]. We are still listening.’ Without breaking the silence of the room.*

**Block 6 — Speaking-Back Round (7:55–8:15, 20 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (60 sec)

*“Time. We move to the speaking-back round. The protocol is on H7.2. Three rules: speak FOR [Bringer], not for yourself. Frame in I-heard / I-sensed language; never ‘God told me you should.’ Pass if nothing specific came; silence is honest.”*

*“Around the circle. About 30 seconds each. Pass anytime. [Bringer], you receive in silence. Notes if helpful. Do not respond during the round; you will weigh and name afterward.”*

*“Cohort Companions, you go first to model the protocol. I will go after the Cohort Companions, then the round opens to everyone.”*

## How the round runs (≈18 min)

**Cohort Companions speak first. Specific. Brief. ‘I heard, [Bringer], that \_\_\_\_\_\_.’ Or: ‘What came to me was \_\_\_\_\_.’ 30 seconds. The model matters; what Cohort Companions speak shapes what listeners offer.**

**Lead Companion speaks next. Same form. 30 seconds.**

**Then the round opens. Around the circle in geographic order; or first-come voluntary; either works. The Lead Companion names which. With 22 listeners and 18 minutes, the math allows about 50 seconds per voice if everyone speaks, or 30 seconds with passes; voluntary order tends to produce truer speakings.**

**If a listener drifts authoritative (‘God told me you should’) the Lead Companion gently interrupts: ‘Say that in I-heard language.’ The listener restates; the round continues.**

**If a listener delivers advice or counsel disguised as hearing, Lead Companion: ‘That sounds like advice. Speak only what came to you while you were listening.’ If the listener cannot find it in I-heard form, they pass.**

**If a listener shares their own parallel-work material rather than speaking FOR the Bringer, Lead Companion gently: ‘That sounds like parallel work for you. Hold it for your standing pair this week. Pass for now.’**

**Toward the end of the round, Lead Companion may invite specific divergence: ‘Was anyone’s hearing different from what has been spoken? Different is not wrong.’**

**If multiple listeners speak the same theme strongly, Lead Companion may name the convergence at the end of the round, without claiming authority: ‘Several of us heard a similar theme around \_\_\_\_\_. The convergence is data; [Bringer] weighs.’**

## Lead Companion script — closing the round (60 sec)

*“(After the last voice or pass.) Round complete. [Bringer], take a breath. We hold space for you to weigh.”*

**Block 7 — Bringer Weighs and Names (8:15–8:23, 8 min)**

## Lead Companion script — opening (30 sec)

*“[Bringer], you have heard from the room. Take 5 minutes in silence to weigh. The cohort sits with you. Then briefly — about 3 minutes — name what landed for you and what you are setting aside. You have explicit permission to set aside any of what was said. The discernment is yours.”*

*“You can also choose to name only that you will weigh privately and report back to your standing pair this week. That is a complete answer if it is the right one for you tonight.”*

## How the weighing runs (≈7.5 min)

**5 minutes silent. Bringer reads notes; reflects; prays briefly internally; weighs.**

**3 minutes Bringer speaks. Possible content: ‘What landed for me was \_\_\_\_\_. I will sit with that further this week.’ ‘What I heard converging from several of you is \_\_\_\_\_ — I want to walk with that in pastoral conversation.’ ‘What I am setting aside is \_\_\_\_\_ — it does not match what I sense the Spirit has been doing.’ ‘I will weigh privately and report to my standing pair.’**

**The cohort listens in silence. No commentary. No additional speaking-back.**

**Lead Companion closes briefly: ‘Thank you, [Bringer]. The room received your question; you weighed; the work continues this week.’**

*Watch for: the Bringer who tries to thank or affirm individual listeners (‘what [name] said was particularly helpful’). Gently redirect: ‘Hold the specific affirmations for offline. Tonight you weigh and name what you are receiving and setting aside.’ The single-listener thanks risks unbalancing the cohort.*

*Watch for: the Bringer who declares immediately (‘I heard exactly what I needed to hear; I am going to act on it tomorrow’). Gently slow: ‘Sit with it across the week. Tonight is the data; the discernment ripens.’ The standing pair conversation this week is the next step.*

*Watch for: the Bringer who names that nothing landed. Honest. ‘That is honest data. The room listened; nothing converged that you can receive tonight; the question may need different shape or different time. Pastoral conversation this week.’*

**Senior teens (15–18)**

**Parents**

## Adjustments

**Parents are most at risk of drifting authoritative. Years of parenting language and church-leadership language can produce ‘God is calling you to’ phrasing without the parent noticing. Lead Companion catches each instance; the team is briefed to expect it.**

**Watch for: the parent whose speaking-back is for a Bringer who is their own teen across cohorts (rare; if the Bringer is a teen with a parent in the room, the team briefs the parent in advance to consider passing in the speaking-back, since cross-family hearing in this format is hard to land cleanly).**

**Watch for: the parent whose speaking-back is for the Bringer who is their own spouse. Same principle; if the Bringer is one spouse, the other spouse is briefed in advance to consider passing or to hold their hearing for the at-home conversation rather than the public round.**

**Watch for: the parent whose speaking-back is heavy with their own life context. ‘I heard what I have been hearing for myself this season — \_\_\_\_\_.’ The parallel may be real; speaking it FOR the Bringer requires confidence that it is for the Bringer specifically. If unsure, pass.**

**Watch for: the parent who tries to offer pastoral counsel in the speaking-back. Lead Companion: ‘Counsel is for offline. Tonight: only what came to you while you were listening, framed in I-heard.’**

**Watch for: the parent Bringer who, when weighing, tries to set aside what was said too quickly because it is uncomfortable. Lead Companion does not push the Bringer to receive; but during their pre-brief Saturday, the Lead Companion can name in advance: ‘Weighing is not the same as setting aside what is hard to hear. **Handout H7.1 — Corporate Listening Prayer Reference**

*From IJH Vol 1 Exp 1 (Word→Hearing→Faith chain in corporate context) and 1 Cor 14:29–33. Carry this card; the practice continues across Wks 7, 8, and 10.*

*Let two or three prophets speak, and let the others weigh what is said. ... For God is not a God of confusion but of peace.*

*— 1 Corinthians 14:29, 33 (ESV)*

**What corporate listening prayer is**

## ONE Bringer with a real present question. The cohort listens together for what the Spirit is saying TO that Bringer ABOUT that question. Each listener speaks back briefly what they heard. The Bringer weighs.

**The architecture protects all parties. The Bringer is not at the mercy of any one listener; the cohort is not the discerner (the Bringer is); no listener carries authority for what they speak.**

**This is corporate prophecy with discernment, in Paul’s 1 Cor 14 sense. Not new; deeply traditional; rare in modern Western practice but central in the New Testament church.**

**The architecture**

## Five movements

**Bringer presents a real present question (4 min).**

**Cohort listens silently, praying for the Bringer (20 min).**

**Speaking-back round: each listener speaks briefly what they heard FOR the Bringer (20 min).**

**Bringer weighs in silence (5 min) and names briefly what landed and what is set aside (3 min).**

**The cohort prays for the Bringer’s walking across the week.**

**Why discernment matters as much as hearing**

## Without discernment, corporate hearing becomes assertion. Three listeners say similar things; a fourth feels obligated to confirm; the Bringer feels obligated to act; spiritual abuse is the architecture’s name when discernment is absent.

**With discernment, corporate hearing becomes formation. Listeners frame in I-heard language; the Bringer weighs; what is from God grows clearer over time and what is human projection thins out under examination.**

**Discernment is a practiced skill, not a feeling. The cohort exercises it together. The standing pair carries the weighing across the week between sessions.**

**Handout H7.2 — The Listener's Protocol**

*For each listener during corporate listening prayer (Wks 7, 10). One page; carry it.*

**Three rules**

## RULE 1 — Listen FOR the Bringer, not for yourself. Your own material may surface during the silent listen; that is parallel work. Speak in the round only what came to you FOR the Bringer.

**RULE 2 — Frame in I-heard / I-sensed language. Never ‘God told me you should.’ Never ‘you must.’ The Bringer weighs.**

**RULE 3 — Pass if nothing specific came. Silence is honest. Filling silence with sentiment is not corporate listening.**

**During the silent listen**

**1.** Pray for the Bringer by name. ‘Father, [Bringer] is asking \_\_\_\_\_. What do you have for them tonight?’

**2.** Sit with the question. Notice what surfaces. Note keywords, images, scriptures, sentences.

**3.** Test what surfaces against the Bringer’s actual question. ‘Does this answer the question they asked? Or am I drifting into giving them general advice?’ Hold what fits; release what doesn’t.

**4.** Test against what you know about the Bringer. ‘Is this consistent with how God has been moving in their life that I have observed? Or is it consistent with how I would want them to change in my preferred direction?’ The first is more often Spirit; the second is more often me.

**5.** If multiple things surface, choose ONE for the speaking-back. The most-clear; the most-specific.

**During the speaking-back**

**1.** Wait your turn. Do not jump in.

**2.** Speak briefly. About 30 seconds. Specific, not abstract.

**3.** Frame in I-heard language. ‘[Bringer], I heard \_\_\_\_.’ Or: ‘What came to me was \_\_\_\_.’ Or: ‘I sensed \_\_\_\_.’

**4.** Do not interpret your own hearing for the Bringer. ‘I heard X, which I think means Y for you’ — stop at X. Y is the Bringer’s discernment.

**5.** Do not give advice in the I-heard wrapper. ‘I heard you should stop being so judgmental of your sister’ is advice. ‘I sensed a softening was being asked of you in a specific relationship’ is hearing.

**6.** Pass if nothing specific came. ‘Pass’ is a complete contribution.

**After the speaking-back**

**1.** Receive in silence with the rest of the cohort while the Bringer weighs.

**2.** Do not approach the Bringer with additional speakings after the session. The round is the round.

**3.** Journal this week what you heard for the Bringer that you did NOT speak. Pray with it.

**4.** If something persists strongly after a week, talk to your standing pair. If it still persists, pastoral 1:1 about whether and how to communicate it.
