## WEEK 2 — WHAT IT MEANS TO BE SENT (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Walk the New Testament architecture of the sent body — Acts 2 (Pentecost; the Spirit empowers the gathered body for outward witness) and 1 Peter 2:9–10 (a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession). Distinguish witness from evangelism, witness from program, witness from performance. Each member begins to discern: what kind of sent-body am I a member of, specifically?

**Anchor scripture.** Acts 2:1–4, 38–41 (Pentecost; the Spirit’s arrival as empowerment for witness); 1 Peter 2:9–10 (the body’s identity as a sent people); Matthew 28:18–20 returning briefly (the great commission read in light of the Acts 2 empowerment, not separate from it).

**Connect focus.** Sent. The architecture of being sent, before the specifics of where each member is sent (which is Wk 3’s work).

**Mode.** Whole-room. The teaching block is shared; the witness exercise (Block 5) is shared; closing is shared.

**Center.** Acts 2 + 1 Peter 2 teaching (20 min). Then a witness exercise: each member, around the circle, briefly names one specific person (initials only) to whom they have been a witness across the past week’s practice (the H1.2 outside-cohort Tell). What was the Tell; what was true about it; what surfaced. The cohort listens together.

**Between-session practice.** Daily noticing question intensifies: ‘Where this week am I already a witness, even unintentionally?’ Continue the weekly Tell outside the cohort. Standing pair check-in this week: ‘What is the kind of sent-body you sense the Spirit is making YOU specifically? Not where you are sent yet — what kind of witness?’

**IJH source.** Acts 2 (Pentecost as the body-empowerment paradigm); 1 Peter 2:9–10 (the corporate identity of the sent body); proposed Vol 1 Exploration on baptism in the Holy Spirit as empowerment for witness (distinct from regeneration); Vol 2 Exp 9 (Community as Amplifier — the body sent together amplifies witness, just as it amplifies hearing).

## WATCH FOR (Week 2 specific risks — read twice)

*The risks tonight are theological-pastoral. The vocabulary of ‘witness’ carries baggage in many traditions; the architecture must hold without collapsing into either evangelism-as-program or witness-as-performance.*

**Witness collapsed into evangelism-as-program. Some members may default to a ‘how to share my faith’ frame they learned elsewhere. The architecture is more specific: witness is the body’s outward expression of what the Spirit has formed in it. Programs are tools; witness is identity. Re-frame gently when this collapse happens.**

**Witness performed as proof of growth. Some members may report this week’s Tell as evidence of their formation. The architecture is the opposite — the witness IS the formation, lived; not its proof.**

**The participant whose week’s Tell did not happen. ‘I forgot to do the Tell.’ ‘There was nobody to Tell.’ ‘I tried but it felt forced.’ All honest. Receive without shame; Going Out re-engages each week. Re-frame the practice: ‘The Tell is not a quota; it is a noticing. Where this week did the Spirit move you toward someone, and what happened?’**

**The participant whose Tell crossed boundaries (was experienced by the receiver as intrusion). The Spirit’s movement does not override relational care; receive the participant’s honest report; pastoral support if welcomed for refining the witness posture.**

**Coercive evangelistic framing. ‘I converted my colleague.’ ‘I told my friend they need Jesus.’ The architecture refuses both directions of misfit — neither domination of the other person’s spiritual journey, nor abdication of what the Spirit has actually given the witness to say.**

**The participant whose tradition has framed witness in a way that shuts down their Going Out engagement. Some members may carry exhaustion or harm from prior evangelism cultures. Receive the wound; Going Out offers a different architecture; pastoral 1:1 if welcomed.**

**The cohort treating witness as inherently outward to non-Christians. The Acts 2 architecture begins with witness AMONG already-committed people (the 120 in the upper room are addressed first; the witness extends from there). Some members’ Going Out witness will be primarily within Christian community as much as beyond it. Both are honest.**

**The participant whose week surfaced significant relational opening (‘my coworker started asking real questions’) before they had architecture to hold it. Receive without rushing. Pastoral 1:1 within the week to walk the unfolding.**

**Generational projection. A parent says ‘our kids today don’t know how to witness.’ A senior teen says ‘our parents do witness wrong.’ Same redirect as in Going Deeper — the witness each member is being formed for is theirs; cross-generational pronouncement is almost always projection.**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 2)

*Crisis risk is low. The architectural risks are larger than the clinical ones.*

**If a participant’s Tell surfaced harm — the receiver experienced it as coercive, manipulative, or violating. Receive the participant’s honest report without rushing. Pastoral 1:1 within the week. The participant may need to do relational repair with the receiver; the team supports without prescribing.**

**If a participant’s Tell revealed material about themselves they had not yet brought into the cohort (a long-standing relational rupture; a vocational mismatch; a faith struggle). Receive without making it the cohort’s focus tonight. Pastoral 1:1 within 48 hours.**

**If a teen’s Tell crossed a safety threshold (a peer disclosed self-harm; a friend disclosed abuse). Section 6 protocol immediately. Mandatory-reporting law applies as relevant.**

**If a participant brings to the cohort a witness exchange that involved someone known to other cohort members. Confidentiality discipline tightens; the participant tells what they need to tell without naming the specific person; pastoral 1:1 for the relational dynamics.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

Acts 2 is the architectural watershed of the New Testament’s sent-body theology. Before Pentecost, the disciples were a body in formation, hidden, waiting. After Pentecost, they were a body in witness, public, sent. The same Spirit who had been with them was now in them; the same body that had been gathered was now empowered to be outward. This is the paradigm. Going Out’s Wk 2 names it explicitly so that Going Out’s outward work has its proper theological grounding.

1 Peter 2:9–10 names the corporate identity of the sent body. ‘A chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession, that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness into his marvellous light.’ The vocabulary is corporate — race, priesthood, nation, people. The witness is corporate — the body proclaims; the witness emerges from the body’s shared identity. This counters the common American Christian default of witness as primarily individual evangelistic act.

Tonight’s teaching distinguishes three things. Witness from evangelism: evangelism is one form of witness, but witness is broader — it includes the body’s lived presence in the world, not only its verbal proclamation. Witness from program: programs are tools; witness is identity. Witness from performance: performance attempts to demonstrate something the witness already is.

The witness exercise (Block 5) makes the abstract concrete. Each member, briefly, names one specific instance from the past week’s practice (H1.2’s outside-cohort Tell) where they were a witness — even if they did not name it as such at the time. The cohort listens. The teaching becomes practice; the practice becomes data; the data informs Wk 3’s discernment of where each member is being sent.

**Teaching points (do not read this verbatim)**

**•** Notice what Acts 2 establishes. The body is gathered (‘all together in one place’). The Spirit’s arrival is corporate (‘filled the entire house’) before it is individual (‘rested on each one of them’). The empowerment is for outward witness — immediately, in the next verses, Peter preaches and three thousand are added. The body and the witness emerge together; they are not separable.

**•** Notice what 1 Peter 2 establishes. The vocabulary is corporate. Race. Priesthood. Nation. People. The witness is corporate — ‘that you may proclaim the excellencies.’ ‘You’ plural, not ‘you’ singular. The body, together, is the unit of witness.

**•** This is the architecture of being sent. Not ‘I personally am sent to convert someone.’ ‘Our body, formed by the Spirit, is sent into the world; my witness emerges from being part of that body.’ The shift in posture is significant; it changes how we hold our weekly Tells, our daily noticing, our specific encounters with people who do not yet share our faith.

## The three distinctions

**WITNESS FROM EVANGELISM. Evangelism is one form of witness; witness is broader. Witness includes the body’s presence in the world, its faithfulness in ordinary work, its love for one another that becomes visible (John 13:35). Evangelistic conversation is part of witness; it does not exhaust it.**

**WITNESS FROM PROGRAM. Programs are tools — a study, an event, a curriculum. Witness is identity — who the body is in the world. Programs serve witness; they do not substitute for it. A program-heavy church can have impoverished witness; a program-light church can have rich witness; the question is identity, not method.**

**WITNESS FROM PERFORMANCE. Performance attempts to demonstrate something the witness already is. Authentic witness has nothing to prove; it is the natural overflow of what the Spirit has formed. Going Deeper’s formation work was specifically against performance — honest assessment, named shadow, real confession. Spring’s witness is the lived overflow of that honest formation.**

**Junior teens (12–14)**

## Adjustments

**Junior teens often experience witness as awkward by default — they have peer dynamics that make any ‘I noticed something about you and want to share something’ conversation feel risky. Receive the awkwardness as data, not failure.**

**Watch for: junior teens whose Tell happened with a parent, sibling, or close friend at home rather than at school. Honour. Witness is not less when it is at home.**

**Watch for: junior teens whose Tell didn’t happen because they couldn’t identify anyone outside the cohort. Re-frame: ‘Family count too. Choir or sports team count too. School friends count even if you did not name what you said as a Tell.’**

**Watch for: junior teens whose Tell crossed into peer disclosure that surfaced something concerning (a friend’s self-harm, a family member’s harm). Receive briefly; pastoral 1:1 immediately after the session.**

**Watch for: junior teens whose framing of witness is shaped by a particular church culture they have inherited (perhaps an evangelistic urgency that doesn’t fit them). The three distinctions help; receive without contradicting their tradition aggressively.**

**Senior teens (15–18)**

## Adjustments

**Senior teens often have more developed witness frames — some from youth ministry exposure, some from academic engagement, some from prior evangelism training. Receive the framing each senior brings; the three distinctions can be used to refine without overriding.**

**Watch for: the senior whose Tell was theologically substantive (‘my friend asked me about the resurrection’). Honour. Specific theological witness is real and Spirit-led.**

**Watch for: the senior whose Tell was relationally substantive without being verbally evangelistic (‘I sat with my friend who lost her grandparent’). Honour. The body’s ministry of presence is witness too.**

**Watch for: the senior whose Tell involved another teen who is in their school context, where the Tell may have ongoing consequences. Pastoral 1:1 within the week to walk what may unfold.**

**Watch for: the senior whose framing is anti-evangelism (a counter-reaction to a tradition they came from). The three distinctions affirm broader witness without dismissing evangelism; the senior may need to hear evangelism as ONE form of witness, not the totality.**

**Parents**

## Adjustments

**Parents often have the broadest witness landscape — colleagues, extended family, neighbours, fellow parents, professional contacts. The diversity of contexts is itself data; receive each parent’s specific landscape.**

**Watch for: the parent whose Tell happened with their adult child not in the cohort, or with their spouse. Honour the family-level witness; the cohort is one of many places witness happens.**

**Watch for: the parent whose Tell revealed an older relational rupture (a former colleague, an estranged family member). Receive without making it the cohort’s focus; pastoral 1:1 if welcomed for the relational complexity.**

**Watch for: the parent whose Tell was vocationally substantive (a witness conversation with a colleague that touched faith and work). Honour; this is often where parents’ most consequential witness happens.**

**Watch for: the parent whose Tell crossed into territory their church culture would not affirm (perhaps a witness to a same-sex couple, perhaps a witness to a family member of another faith). Receive without prescribing; pastoral support if welcomed.**

**Watch for: the parent who had no Tell because their week was simply consumed (a child illness, a vocational crisis, an aging parent). Honour. Some weeks the witness is simply faithful presence in difficult conditions.**

*When the day of Pentecost arrived, they were all together in one place. And suddenly there came from heaven a sound like a mighty rushing wind, and it filled the entire house where they were sitting.*

**What Acts 2 establishes**

## The body is gathered first — ‘all together in one place.’ The Spirit’s arrival presupposes the gathered body.

**The empowerment is corporate before individual — ‘filled the entire house’ then ‘rested on each one of them.’ The body is the unit that receives.**

**The empowerment is for outward witness — immediately Peter preaches, three thousand are added. Empowerment is not separable from witness.**

**The body of the witnesses forms (Acts 2:42–47) — teaching, fellowship, breaking of bread, prayers, daily life shared. Witness produces formation; formation produces witness.**

**What 1 Peter 2 establishes**

## The vocabulary is corporate. Race, priesthood, nation, people. Not ‘individual believer’; ‘body together.’

**The witness is corporate. ‘That YOU may proclaim’ — plural. The body proclaims; individual members participate in the body’s proclamation.**

**The identity is given before the witness is enacted. ‘You ARE a chosen race’ — not ‘you must become.’ The witness emerges from the identity.**

**The body is itself a sign. ‘Once you were not a people, but now you are God’s people; once you had not received mercy, but now you have received mercy.’ The transformation of the body is part of the witness.**

**Why this matters for Going Out**

The American Christian default is witness as primarily individual evangelistic act — one believer sharing faith with one non-believer. Acts 2 and 1 Peter 2 do not deny that this happens; they ground it in something larger. Witness is the body’s outward life. Each member’s witness emerges from being part of a sent body — a body that has been formed by the Spirit, that lives a particular kind of life together, and whose members carry that body’s life into their daily contexts.

Practically: when you Tell someone outside the cohort this week, you are not primarily a single witness; you are part of a body that has formed you and goes with you. The body’s prayer is around you; the body’s formation is in you; the body’s witness is what you participate in. This frees the witness from the loneliness and pressure of being the only voice; it grounds it in something larger.

**Handout H2.3 — The Three Frames Card**

*Pocket-sized reference. Print on cardstock; carry in wallet or planner. The three frames refine witness across Going Out.*

**Three frames for refining witness**

## Witness from evangelism

**Evangelism is one form of witness. Witness includes: presence in the world; faithfulness in ordinary work; love among the body that becomes visible; specific words spoken when the Spirit gives them; specific actions taken when the moment calls.**

**If your only frame for witness is evangelism, witness will feel rare and high-pressure. If your frame is broader, witness happens daily in ordinary form.**

## Witness from program

**Programs are tools — a study, an event, a curriculum, a structure. Witness is the body’s identity in the world. Programs serve witness; they do not substitute for it.**

**If your only frame for witness is program-driven (‘we did the witness event last quarter’), the body’s daily life may not actually be witnessing. If your frame is identity-driven, witness is happening even between programs.**

## Witness from performance

**Performance attempts to demonstrate something the witness already is. Authentic witness has nothing to prove; it is the natural overflow of what the Spirit has formed.**

**If you find yourself performing witness, return to the formation. Going Deeper named soils, knots, lies, gifts, shadow. Spring’s witness is the lived overflow of that honest formation. When the witness is forced, the formation needs renewed attention.**
