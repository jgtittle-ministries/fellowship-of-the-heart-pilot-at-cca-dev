## WEEK 8 — SPECIFIC MISSIONAL ENGAGEMENT (90 minutes)

**Aim.** Walk the Acts 13 corporate-listening architecture as a cohort. Discern what (if anything) the Spirit is inviting the cohort — as a body — into beyond what each member is doing individually. The discipline is the same as Going Deeper Wk 10’s discernment for one Discerner; tonight’s discerner is the cohort itself, with each member contributing what surfaced in the standing-pair preparation conversation.

**Anchor scripture.** Acts 13:1–3 (the Antioch sending paradigm); 1 Corinthians 14:29 (‘let two or three prophets speak, and let the others weigh what is said’); Acts 15:28 (‘it has seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us’ — the Jerusalem council as the body’s discernment of corporate direction).

**Connect focus.** Mission far. Cohort-level discernment of corporate engagement.

**Mode.** Whole-room. The cohort sits as one body — the unit of discernment IS the cohort, not the individual. The Lead Companion holds the architecture; each member contributes; the body weighs together.

**Center.** Brief teaching frame (10 min). 60-second silent settling and corporate prayer. Each member shares around the circle what surfaced in the past week’s standing-pair check-in (‘What might the Spirit be inviting our COHORT into beyond what each of us is doing alone?’) — 60 sec each, captured on flip chart (35 min). The cohort weighs what was heard — patterns, convergences, divergences (15 min). The body lands honestly: a specific shared engagement, or no shared engagement this season, or a clarifying question for further discernment (15 min).

**Between-session practice.** Whatever the cohort discerned tonight is integrated through the week. If a specific shared engagement was named: each member sits with it; the standing pair walks the cost of yes; the body re-engages at Wk 9. If no shared engagement: each member returns to individual sent-context, with the cohort’s honest discernment as the foundation. Daily noticing question: ‘What is the body of cohort-witnesses asking of me this week?’ Standing-pair check-in: ‘Where do you land on what the cohort named?’

**IJH source.** Acts 13:1–3 (Antioch sending); 1 Cor 14:29 (community testing of what is heard); Vol 5 Barnabas (the body that worshipped, fasted, and was sent — the architectural anchor); Vol 2 Exp 9 (Community as Amplifier); Vol 2 Exp 10 (the five-level group taxonomy — tonight is a Level 4 attempt, the body hearing for the body); Going Deeper Wk 10 (the Acts 13 discernment for one Discerner, scaled tonight to the cohort itself).

## WATCH FOR (Week 8 specific risks — read twice)

*Tonight’s risks center on the architectural integrity of corporate discernment. The cohort can either do real Level 4 work — the body hearing for the body — or it can drift into Level 3 (good work together but not corporate hearing) or even Level 2 (social fellowship that produces consensus without genuine listening). The Lead Companion’s architectural discipline is what holds the difference.*

**Premature consensus. Cohort members converge quickly on a single proposal that emerged from a few strong contributors’ pair conversations. The Lead Companion’s discipline: weigh, do not vote. Ask explicitly for divergent voices. The body that votes is doing committee work; the body that weighs is doing discernment. The Vol 2 Exp 9 gnostic-community danger is operative tonight: a cohort that has formed deeply across Going Deeper can drift into closed self-validation, producing false consensus that feels like Spirit-confirmation but is actually the cohort hearing itself. The Lead Companion’s discipline is to name when this is happening.**

**Coercive contributions. A member presents their pair-discerned possibility as if it were prophetic mandate (‘I really feel the Lord wants us to \_\_\_’). The architecture from Going Deeper Wk 7 corporate listening applies: contributions are humble offerings the body weighs, not prophetic decrees. Lead Companion redirects gently when needed.**

**Project-thinking. The cohort drifts toward planning a service event rather than discerning the Spirit’s call. Re-frame: tonight is not project planning. If a specific engagement is named, Wk 9 walks the cost; if no engagement is named, that is honest data. Project planning happens AFTER discernment, not as discernment.**

**Activism collapse. The cohort defaults to a generic ‘let’s do something for [population in need]’ without specific Spirit-direction. The Acts 13 paradigm is specific (‘Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them’), not generic. Tonight’s discernment, if it lands on a shared engagement, should be specific.**

**Honest no. The cohort may discern that the Spirit is NOT calling them to a specific cohort-level engagement this season — each member is sent into their individual sent-context, and the cohort’s spring work is supporting one another’s individual engagements rather than launching a shared one. Lead Companion frames at the start: this is an honest possible outcome.**

**The dominant voice. One or two members’ strong proposals dominate the contribution time, crowding out quieter voices. Lead Companion floats the room with attention; explicit invitations to specific cohorts (‘Junior teens, what surfaced for you in the pair conversation?’) at need.**

**Cross-cohort family material. A teen and parent in different cohorts had separate pair conversations whose outputs intersect. The cohort space holds without adjudication; cross-cohort dynamics are managed by Companions privately.**

**Member who feels they cannot say no. A member senses the cohort moving toward an engagement they are not personally able to participate in (life-stage, capacity, conviction). The architecture protects: contributions are not commitments; the cohort’s discernment of direction is separate from any individual member’s ability to participate.**

**Member who feels they are the only ‘right’ voice. A member senses they have heard accurately and the cohort is not converging. The Vol 2 Jeremiah/Noah exception applies — the prophetic voice may stand alone — but the standard is high (specific, scripturally-grounded, held up for testing not asserted against testing, patient enough to wait for natural vindicating event). Lead Companion may name this category honestly.**

## CRISIS CONTINGENCIES (Week 8)

*Crisis risk is moderate. The architectural risks are larger than clinical ones. The team’s discipline is holding the architecture without forcing a particular outcome.*

**If the cohort cannot agree what was heard. Lead Companion holds the architecture: ‘We have heard divergent contributions. The body has not converged. That is honest data; we hold the question open for further discernment.’ Wk 9 may revisit; Going Out may close without cohort-level engagement; both outcomes are honest.**

**If a member experiences acute distress during the discernment (the body landing on an engagement they cannot participate in; the body NOT landing on what the member needed it to land on; the cohort’s discernment surfacing a personal call the member is unprepared for). Receive the distress; pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours.**

**If the cohort’s discernment surfaces material involving a specific population in need (a refugee community, a homeless-services partnership, a justice-related engagement) that requires institutional partnership: the cohort space discerns direction, not partnership specifics; pastoral / leadership conversation outside the session for partnership logistics.**

**If a senior teen’s pair-discerned possibility involves engagement beyond their parents’ ability to consent (overnight travel, financial commitment, geographic relocation): cross-cohort pastoral conversation outside the session; the cohort space honors the senior’s discernment without making the parent the obstacle.**

**If the discernment surfaces a politically-coded engagement (advocacy work; specific cause aligned with one political tradition): Lead Companion’s discipline is the witness posture, not adjudication of the political content. The cohort can discern direction; the political coding is held with care.**

**Session at a Glance**

**Why this session, this week**

Vol 2’s five-level group taxonomy (Exp 10) names Level 4 as ‘a body that hears together’ — the cohort develops agency; the Spirit speaks to it as a unit. Most groups never reach Level 4; reaching it is hard. Going Deeper Wk 8 was the cohort’s first Level 4 attempt (the cohort listening for what the Spirit was saying ABOUT the cohort). Tonight is the cohort’s second Level 4 attempt: listening for what the Spirit is saying THROUGH the cohort about its corporate engagement in mission far.

Acts 13:1–3 is the architectural anchor. The Antioch body, gathered for ordinary work (worship and fasting), heard specific Spirit direction (‘Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them’). The body confirmed (more fasting and praying), then sent. The pattern: ordinary corporate practice produces capacity for specific corporate hearing; the body weighs together; the body sends with confirmation. Going Deeper Wk 10 walked one Discerner through this architecture; tonight scales it to the cohort itself.

The 1 Corinthians 14:29 discipline applies: ‘let two or three prophets speak, and let the others weigh what is said.’ The cohort’s discernment is not a vote; it is weighing. Each contribution is a humble offering; the body together tests what was heard against scripture, against the body’s formation, against observable fruit. The Vol 2 gnostic-community danger looms: a body that closes its feedback loop and treats internal consensus as self-validating ceases to amplify and starts deceiving itself. Tonight’s discipline is openness to the body’s honest weighing, including honest divergence.

The honest range of outcomes is wide. The cohort may discern a specific shared engagement (cohort-level mission emerges; Wk 9 walks the cost; Going Out closes with the body sent into corporate work). The cohort may discern that there is no specific shared engagement this season — each member is sent into the direction their individual discernment has confirmed: a calling to start a new group, a vocational deepening, a household formation season, a third-place sustained arc, a one-to-one accompaniment, or a contemplative/hidden shape. This second landing is not a default-when-no-shared-engagement-emerges; it is the genuine confirmation of differentiated callings the body honors equally. The cohort may discern that the question needs further work (a clarifying question for the team's pastoral attention; Wk 9 may revisit). All three are honest possible outcomes.

**Teaching points**

**•** Notice what Acts 13 establishes. The body is gathered for ORDINARY work (worship and fasting) when the Spirit speaks specifically. The Spirit’s direction is specific (named individuals, named work). The body confirms (more fasting and praying) before sending. Direction, confirmation, sending. Tonight’s discernment follows the same shape.

**•** Notice what 1 Cor 14 establishes. Corporate hearing requires corporate weighing. Two or three prophets speak (not one); the others weigh (not vote). The body together tests what was heard. The discipline is humble offering and corporate testing, not declaration and assent.

**•** In Going Deeper Wk 10, ONE Discerner walked the Acts 13 architecture for an emerging individual calling. Tonight, the COHORT walks the architecture. The discerner-receiver tonight is the cohort itself; each member contributes what surfaced in pair conversation; the body weighs together; the body lands honestly.

**TWO — The body has prepared (the standing-pair check-in question across the past week).**

## How the block runs

**Lead Companion goes first; models the architecture in 60 seconds.**

**One Cohort Companion goes second.**

**Around the circle. Each member 60 seconds. With 24 members, that’s about 25 minutes; with extra time for transitions.**

**Lead Companion captures contributions on the flip chart in the LEFT column (what was heard). Specific phrases drawn from the contributions.**

**If a member contribution drifts into prophetic decree mode (‘the Lord wants us to’): brief redirect after the contribution. ‘We hold what was named as one offering. The body will weigh.’**

**If a member surfaces an explicitly politicized possibility: receive cleanly without commentary; the cohort’s weighing will engage it.**

**If multiple members converge on similar contributions: capture without merging prematurely. The convergence is data the weighing block will engage.**

**If a member contribution reveals concerning material (a personal calling beyond their capacity, a coercive dynamic with another cohort member, a politicized contribution that breaks the cohort’s peace): receive briefly; flag for pastoral 1:1 within 24 hours; do not adjudicate in the cohort space.**

**Block 5 — Cohort Weighing (8:00–8:15, 15 min)**

## Lead Companion script

*“What the flip chart shows. Let me read what we heard.”*

*(Lead Companion reads contributions from the flip chart, briefly. Without commentary on each.)*

*“The body weighs. Three questions guide the weighing.”*

*“ONE — What patterns are visible? Where did contributions converge?”*

*“TWO — Where are honest divergences? Where did contributions point in different directions, or where does what we heard not ring true to some of us?”*

*“THREE — What is the body together hearing about itself — is the Spirit naming a specific shared engagement, or naming that we are sent as individuals this season?”*

*“Contributions are voluntary. Brief. The body weighs together; we are not yet landing.”*

## During the weighing

**Voluntary contributions; receive without commentary on each.**

**Lead Companion captures observations in the RIGHT column of the flip chart (how it lands).**

**Critical: the Lead Companion explicitly invites divergent voices. ‘Where does what we heard NOT ring true to some of you?’ Premature consensus is the chief architectural risk; explicit invitation to divergence guards against it.**

**If a strong voice begins to dominate the weighing, gentle redirect: ‘Let me hear from someone who hasn’t weighed yet.’**

**If the cohort is silent or hesitant, the Lead Companion does NOT fill the silence with their own weighing. The silence is the body taking its time. Up to 30 seconds of silence is appropriate; if longer, the Lead may invite specifically: ‘Junior teens, what do you sense?’**

**The Lead Companion is NOT trying to drive toward consensus. The Lead is holding the weighing process. If the body diverges, the divergence is the data.**

**Differentiation by Cohort**

*Tonight there is no cohort split. Cohort Companions read their own cohort’s members across the session.*

**Junior teens (12–14)**

## Adjustments

**Junior teens often hold corporate-discernment work less easily than parents. Their pair conversations may have produced concrete-relational possibilities (‘our cohort should serve at the homeless shelter’) without the discernment texture the architecture asks for. Receive cleanly; the cohort’s weighing will engage what each contribution actually is.**

**Watch for: junior teens whose contribution echoes a parent’s contribution. Cross-cohort awareness; honour the teen’s contribution; the weighing will sort what is genuinely the teen’s and what is family-system echo.**

**Watch for: junior teens whose pair conversation produced honest blank. ‘My pair partner and I didn’t come up with anything.’ Receive as honest data; the body holds without grading.**

**Watch for: junior teens whose contribution surfaces a peer-context engagement (‘we should reach out to the kids at our school who are struggling’). Receive; pastoral attention to specifics outside the session.**

**Senior teens (15–18)**

## Adjustments

**Senior teens often arrive with developed missional vocabulary; their contributions may be theologically articulate. Receive cleanly; the body’s weighing applies to senior contributions as much as to anyone’s.**

**Watch for: the senior whose contribution names a specific cause aligned with their academic or vocational direction (justice work, climate engagement, refugee support). Receive; the cohort weighs whether this is a senior’s personal calling or a possible cohort engagement.**

**Watch for: the senior whose contribution reveals tension with their parents’ likely contributions (politically-coded engagement, advocacy work, justice frame). The cohort space holds the senior’s discernment; cross-cohort dynamics handled outside.**

**Watch for: the senior carrying the Wk 10 Discerner role from Going Deeper whose calling-integration has reached a moment where the cohort is being asked to support their personal sending rather than be sent together. Receive cleanly.**

**Parents**

## Adjustments

**Parents often have the most developed corporate-discernment capacity, having been part of more bodies that have done corporate work. Their contributions may carry weight; they may also dominate. Lead Companion floats with attention.**

**Watch for: the parent whose contribution echoes a long-running personal interest that they are projecting onto the cohort (‘our cohort should \_\_\_\_\_’ when the parent has been doing \_\_\_\_\_ for years personally). Receive without dismissing; the weighing will sort.**

**Watch for: the parent in vocational position to drive a cohort engagement (board membership, ministry leadership, professional connection). Honour while watching for the dominance dynamic; the cohort’s discernment is the cohort’s, not the connected parent’s.**

**Watch for: the parent whose contribution names a specific population they have professional experience with (clinical, educational, social services). The professional knowledge is real and useful; the discernment is whether the cohort is being called there.**

**Watch for: the parent in life-stage that limits cohort engagement participation (caregiving, intensive parenting, professional crisis). The contribution may be specific direction, with explicit acknowledgment that the parent themselves cannot participate. Honour both the contribution and the constraint.**

**Companion Team Debrief Prompts**

The team meets the day after Wk 8 for a 75-minute debrief (longer because of architectural risk).

**Signs the session worked**

**•** The contributions block produced honest range; coercive contributions did not dominate.

**•** The weighing block produced genuine weighing — patterns named, divergences acknowledged, no premature consensus.

**•** The landing was honest — whatever the body landed on was what the body actually heard, not what the team wanted.

**•** Honest divergent voices were invited explicitly and surfaced.

**•** By Friday, members report carrying the landed sentence into the week is producing real noticings.

**Signs the session did not work as well**

**•** Premature consensus emerged in the weighing block; the cohort moved too quickly toward a single landing.

**•** Coercive contributions dominated; the body’s honest divergence was suppressed.

**•** The Lead Companion drove toward a particular outcome rather than holding the weighing.

**•** A member’s acute material (calling crisis, cross-cohort family rupture, politicized engagement) was inadequately handled.

**•** The architecture drifted toward project planning; the discernment never actually happened.

**Handout H8.2 — Acts 13 Cohort-Scaled Architecture**

*The Acts 13 paradigm scaled from one Discerner (Going Deeper Wk 10) to the cohort itself.*

*While they were worshiping the Lord and fasting, the Holy Spirit said, ‘Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them.’ Then after fasting and praying they laid their hands on them and sent them off.*

*— Acts 13:2–3 (ESV)*

**The four-step Acts 13 architecture, scaled**

## ONE — The body is gathered. The cohort’s ordinary Tuesday rhythm.

**TWO — The body is in ordinary spiritual practice. Worship and fasting (Antioch); the cohort’s pair-prepared discernment plus optional fasting (us).**

**THREE — The Spirit speaks specifically. ‘Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them’ (Antioch); whatever the cohort’s contributions and weighing surface (us).**

**FOUR — The body confirms before sending. More fasting and praying (Antioch); Wk 9’s walk-the-cost or individual-sending preparation (us).**

**Differences from Going Deeper Wk 10**

## Wk 10’s Discerner was ONE individual the body was discerning for. Tonight’s discerner is the cohort itself; each member contributes; the body weighs.

**Wk 10’s 18-minute surfacing block is replaced tonight by the 35-minute contributions and the 15-minute weighing. The same architectural function; longer because the body is the discerner.**

**Wk 10’s laying-on-of-hands sending is delayed to Wk 9. Tonight is direction-discernment; Wk 9 is confirmation and sending preparation.**

**Three honest possible landings**

## LANDING 1 — Specific shared engagement. The Spirit is naming a particular work for the cohort.

**LANDING 2 — Individual sending. The Spirit is naming that the cohort is sent as individuals; mutual support is the cohort’s shared work.**

**LANDING 3 — Clarifying question. The discernment is incomplete; the body carries a question rather than a direction.**

*All three are honest outcomes from real corporate listening. Going Out does not require landing 1.*

**Handout H8.3 — Discernment Disciplines Card**

*The disciplines that hold corporate discernment honest. Carry this card across the Going Out series; the disciplines also apply beyond Wk 8.*

**Five disciplines from 1 Cor 14:29 + Vol 2 Exp 9**

## ONE — HUMBLE OFFERING, NOT PROPHETIC DECREE. ‘What surfaced for me was \_\_\_\_’, not ‘the Lord told me we should \_\_\_\_.’ The body weighs.

**TWO — WEIGHING, NOT VOTING. The body tests what was heard against scripture, against formation, against observable fruit. Voting is committee work; weighing is discernment.**

**THREE — EXPLICIT INVITATION TO DIVERGENCE. Premature consensus is the chief architectural risk. ‘Where does what we heard NOT ring true?’ is a required question.**

**FOUR — OPENNESS TO HONEST NO. The body may discern that the Spirit is NOT calling them to specific shared engagement this season. That is honest data, not failure.**

**FIVE — GUARD AGAINST GNOSTIC CLOSURE. From Vol 2: a body that closes its feedback loop and treats internal consensus as self-validating ceases to amplify and starts deceiving itself. Three guards: submission to scripture; openness to external correction; observable fruit as the final test.**

**The Jeremiah / Noah exception**

## When the prophetic voice may stand alone

**Vol 2 names the exception: occasionally a single member may have heard accurately while the body has not. The standard for standing alone is high.**

**FOUR REQUIREMENTS. Specific (not vague impression); scripturally-grounded (resonant with the broader witness of scripture, not pulled from a proof-text); held up for testing rather than asserted against testing; patient enough to wait for the natural vindicating event.**

*Most contributions tonight will not meet this standard, and that is appropriate. The disciple who claims it lightly is almost always wrong; the disciple who waits patiently for it is almost always right when it does come.*

**If the body has not converged**

## What honest divergence means

**The body has not yet heard clearly. That is data, not failure. The discernment continues.**

**Wk 9 may revisit the question. The team’s pastoral attention engages it across the week. Some questions are seasons; some land in subsequent meetings; some never land at the cohort level (they land for individuals instead).**

**The cohort’s honesty is itself the formation. A body that holds an honest ‘we have not yet heard clearly’ is healthier than a body that votes itself into pretended consensus.**
