# What PROAPT is

**PROAPT is a hearing practice, not a Bible study technique.** The distinction matters.

- A Bible study asks: *what does this passage mean?*
- PROAPT asks: *what is the Spirit saying through this passage to me, today, in my specific life?*

Both are valuable. PROAPT is the second.

The operational verse for the practice is **Romans 10:17 (ESV)**:

> *"So faith comes from hearing, and hearing through the word of Christ."*

The chain is **Word → Hearing → Faith**. PROAPT is the simplest practice that works the chain. One short passage a day is enough to start.

# The six steps

The acronym matters because the order matters.

## P — Pray

Invite the Spirit to speak before reading. You are approaching the Word as someone who expects to *hear*, not just to extract information.

*Common error:* skipping it. "I'll just dive in."

## R — Read

Read the passage. To receive, not to analyze yet. Let it land first.

*Common error:* going straight to interpretation before the passage has had time to land.

## O — Observe

What do you actually notice? What stands out? What is strange or unexpected? Honest observation precedes interpretation.

*Common error:* importing what you already think the passage means.

## A — Apply

What does this mean for *you*, today, *specifically*? This is where the general word (the *logos*) becomes a specific word (the *rhema*).

*Common error:* applying the passage to other people.

## P — Pray again

Bring the application back to God. Tell Him what you heard, what you are willing to do, what you are struggling to be willing to do.

*Common error:* skipping it. The obedience channel opens here.

## T — Tell

Share what you heard with someone — a partner, your cohort circle, a spouse, a friend. The speaking solidifies the hearing and creates accountability.

*Common error:* skipping it. The most-often-omitted step. The Tell is what makes the hearing land in the world rather than evaporate by morning.

# How to use the card

- **Print this page** on cardstock. Tuck it into your Bible or your Personal Heart Journal.
- **One short passage at a time.** A paragraph from the gospels, a single Psalm, a half-chapter of an epistle. PROAPT works best with bite-sized passages where one Spirit-given specific can actually land.
- **Daily during weeks 6–10 of Getting Started.** The Personal Heart Journal has a daily PROAPT template on the Week 6 and Week 7 pages.
- **Sustain into the rhythm afterward.** The Rhythm Card lists PROAPT as part of the daily / weekly hearing practice the cohort sustains after Getting Started ends.

---

# When the practice goes flat

Most participants experience the practice going flat at some point — a week of going through the steps and nothing landing. When that happens:

- **Don't quit.** Flat seasons are part of the rhythm. The faculty of hearing develops across years, not weeks.
- **Change the passage type.** If you've been in epistles, move to a gospel or a Psalm. If you've been in narrative, sit with a single proverb.
- **Don't skip the Tell.** Even if the hearing feels thin, name what you have to a partner. The naming often clarifies what was actually there.
- **Stay short.** Reduce to one verse a day for a week. Resist the urge to lengthen the passage to "get something."
- **Bring it to the Companion team.** A flat season that lasts more than two weeks is worth surfacing in the cohort circle. The team can hold it with you.
