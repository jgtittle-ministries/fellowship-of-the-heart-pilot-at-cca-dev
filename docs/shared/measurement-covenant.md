# Why a covenant

Unlike every other card in this folder, this one is not for participants. It is for the people who will *hold* what the participants entrust to us — the surveys, the pulse checks, the observation notes, the trackers named in each series handbook's Assessment Plan. Those instruments are light by design, but they all touch the same material: the condition of a heart that trusted us enough to answer honestly. Knowledge of hearts, in institutional hands, does not stay neutral on its own. It drifts — toward sorting, then managing, then controlling — and the drift happens slowly enough that everyone involved still believes they are shepherding. Written limits are what hold. So before the first instrument is used, the team signs this covenant; and each year, before it is used again, the team re-signs it with the ink of a fresh decision.

One sentence governs everything below, and it is the test each signer accepts personally: **measure nothing you will not weep over.** If you can look at what an instrument shows you about the souls in your care and feel nothing — if a hard result reads to you as a data problem rather than a grief — then you are, in that season, a standoff scientist and not a shepherd, and the measures should live in other hands until your heart is back in the room. This is not a slur on scientists; detachment is a virtue in a laboratory. It is a disqualification in a flock. The Lord Himself delivered the most severe diagnostic verdict in the Gospels — and He delivered it weeping (Luke 19:41–44). That is the posture this covenant binds us to: measurement as an act of love, held by people close enough to the faces to cry over them.

# The commitments

The first seven are the Guardrails of [*A Church Prepared for Revival*](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/a-church-prepared-for-revival-a-pilot-proposed/reader.html#docs/movement-ii-what-is-your-soil/03-the-guardrails.md), adopted whole for this fellowship and restated here in signable form; read that chapter for the full argument under each. The last three are this fellowship's own extensions.

1. **Mirrors, not levers.** Anything we learn about a heart is held *for* that person, never over them. It is not market research, a performance dashboard, or raw material for managing anyone.

2. **No scores.** Nothing about anyone's interior life is ever graded, ranked, or tied to money, position, platform, or standing — because the moment a spiritual measure carries a reward, people will perform it, and the honest will learn to hide.

3. **Consent and daylight.** No one's heart is discussed, recorded, or reported upward without their knowledge and consent. Anyone may decline any instrument, decline without penalty, and change their mind later. If we want to understand someone, we sit down with them, in the open, and ask.

4. **The least-powerful test.** Any honest reading of our results must include the view from the least powerful seat in the room — and if no one at the table knows how this fellowship looks from that seat, the honest finding is *we do not yet know*.

5. **The bad-news test.** Anyone — participant, parent, teen, Companion — must be able to say *this instrument is hurting someone* or *this isn't working*, about these very tools, and be honored for saying it. We will ask ourselves annually: what happened to the last person who told us something we did not want to hear?

6. **Wounds are not weeds.** Low warmth on any instrument is never automatically read as low commitment; some numbness is injury, not immaturity. No instrument response ever licenses pressing into a person's pain uninvited. The person sets the pace of their own disclosure, always.

7. **The shepherd's scale.** Names stop with the person who knows the faces and prays for them — the Companion for their circle, the pastor for the flock. Whatever travels above that level travels *stripped of names* — patterns, never people — and it travels in one register: as material for prayer and lament, not for management.

8. **What the measures cannot see.** Our instruments read *degree* — practice, engagement, growth in what is already alive. They cannot read *kind*. No survey detects the new birth; tended ground must never be mistaken for new ground, and nothing we record is ever treated as a verdict on whether a person belongs to the Lord. That knowledge is His alone: "the LORD sees not as man sees... the LORD looks on the heart" (1 Sam. 16:7, ESV). Likewise no instrument sees a dark night rightly — before any low reading is even *privately* interpreted, the [Signs Card](signs-card.md)'s question comes first: which way does the desire point?

9. **The sent are not lost.** When someone leaves this fellowship *outward* — to serve, to plant, to carry the pattern into another room, another church, another city — our counting records fruit, not loss. A fellowship that measures itself only by who stays will quietly learn to stop sending, and sending is the point (Going Out exists to produce exactly these departures). The grain of wheat that leaves the barn is not missing inventory.

10. **Renewal or lapse.** Every instrument in use is brought back to the table once a year: re-explained, re-consented, and re-signed under this covenant — or retired. No survey, tracker, or note-taking practice continues by momentum. Wineskins stiffen; this is how we keep ours supple, including the one this covenant is written on.

# Where this covenant comes from

The commitments above were not invented cautiously in advance; they were demanded by review. The formation documents beneath this fellowship were examined in a twelve-part foundational review, and its most persistent structural finding was precisely this page's subject: measurement's relationship to power must be *designed*, in writing, before the first instrument is used — because sincerity does not protect the vulnerable; written limits do. The full trail, and the research program these commitments feed, live in the IJH [Research Register](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart/reader.html#docs/volume-6-governance/research-register.md). The pastoral heart of it fits in one line, and it is the line this covenant asks you to sign your name under:

**Measure nothing you will not weep over.**
