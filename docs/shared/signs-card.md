*When the practices go dry — four kinds of dry, and how to tell them apart*

# Why this card exists

Sooner or later, everyone who works the [Rhythm Card](rhythm-card.md) hits a season where the practices go gray. Prayer feels like talking to the ceiling. Scripture reads like cardboard. The garden is just a chair.

When that season comes, the most dangerous thing in the room is not the dryness. It is the *wrong diagnosis* — the voice (sometimes another person's, usually your own) that says dryness means failure, or fake faith, or a God who looked at you and left. Whole journeys have ended over a misread dry season. This card exists so that never happens to anyone in this fellowship.

Dryness is usually the tide, and only sometimes the thorn. The tide goes out and comes back; that is how seas work, and how souls work. This card is for telling the tide from the things that are not the tide.

# The one question that sorts everything

**Which way does the desire point?**

Not *how do you feel* — feelings are exactly what have gone quiet. The question is underneath the feelings: when you are honest, do you still *want* God — do you miss Him, does the distance ache — or has the wanting itself faded and the world tastes fine without Him?

That single question separates four different kinds of dry, and they need different responses. Two of them even command *opposite* motions, which is why this card must be learned before the dry season and not improvised inside it.

# The four kinds of dry

| | **1. Neglect** | **2. Worn out** | **3. The enemy's dry wind** | **4. God's own stripping** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Desire for God** | Faded; the distance doesn't ache | Buried under duty; you want escape, not God | Under assault; you feel accused and pressured | Intact and starving — the ache is the sign |
| **Rest of life** | The world still tastes fine | Restless churn; "anywhere but here" | Urgency, fear, secrecy | The world has gone gray too; nothing else satisfies either |
| **The practices** | Quietly lapsed, and you don't grieve them | Frantic or duty-driven, joyless | You're tempted to quit and undo good decisions | You keep showing up, and it feels like nothing |
| **The tell** | No ache at all | The "I'd be better off elsewhere" whisper | A voice counseling change: *quit, undo, leave* | Painful worry about failing God — lukewarm hearts never worry about that |
| **First step** | Wake and turn; rebuild the rhythm (James 4:8) | Rest; lighter yoke; stay put; smaller faithful steps (Matt. 11:28–30) | **Resist**: change nothing, pray a little more, tell someone (1 Pet. 5:8–9) | **Receive**: wait, stay faithful, stay accompanied (Job 23:10) |

Notice cases 3 and 4: one says *fight*, the other says *yield*. Getting them backwards — fighting God's stripping, or yielding to the enemy's wind — is the costliest mistake in the dry country. When you cannot tell which one you are in, do not decide alone. That is what Companions are for.

# The rules of the card

1. **This card is for your own heart, and for one trusted person to help you read it.** It is never a tool for diagnosing somebody else's soul at a distance. Nobody gets filed under a column.
2. **In every case, tell someone.** Darkness spoken aloud loses half its weight. Dryness that stays hidden is the kind the research says goes bad; dryness that is spoken, met without a verdict, and companioned is the kind that becomes root system.
3. **Make no big decisions in the dark.** No quitting, no leaving, no undoing of things decided in the light — until the light returns.
4. **Know when to call the doctor.** If the gray has swallowed everything — sleep, appetite, humor, compassion — or there is self-loathing or any talk of self-harm, that is a treatable illness, not a spiritual verdict. Licensed care and faithful companionship cooperate; they do not compete. Getting help is obedience.

# If you are the dry one

Hear this plainly: **the fact that His absence hurts you is the evidence that He has not gone.** Drifting hearts do not ache. Abandoned fields do not thirst. "My soul thirsts for you; my flesh faints for you, as in a dry and weary land where there is no water" (Psalm 63:1, ESV) is not the prayer of a person God has discarded — the Bible holds it up as the prayer of a person God is drawing.

So do not grade yourself by your feelings; God never did. Keep showing up — badly, dryly, five honest minutes at a time, the way the [Rhythm Card](rhythm-card.md) already taught you: begin again on the next available day. You walk a road that Teresa of Calcutta, Charles Spurgeon, the psalmists, and a great cloud of the dry-but-faithful walked before you — and the record of their lives says the darkness kept coming right before the fruit.
