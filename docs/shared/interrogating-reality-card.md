# What this is

A **corporate knot** is something a cohort (or a family, or a church) already senses but has — without ever deciding to — agreed not to say aloud: the gap between what we profess and how we treat each other, the fear no one names, the thing everyone is making up stories about. It is the *between-us* cousin of the individual knots the Going Deeper work already addresses one heart at a time.

This card is the practice for naming such a thing **safely**, locating it, and bringing it to the Lord together. It is the reusable, portable form of what **Week 8 — the cohort hears itself** does live, and it leans on the same I-heard discernment discipline the cohort learned in **Week 7 — corporate listening prayer**.

**Anchor scripture.** *"Buy the truth and do not sell it"* (Prov 23:23). The body is one with many members, and each sees in part (1 Cor 12:12–27; 1 Cor 13:9–12). *"Speaking the truth in love"* (Eph 4:15; cf. Matt 18:15). *"Be still, and know"* (Ps 46:10). Where two or three are gathered, He is in the midst (Matt 18:20); and the others weigh what is heard (1 Cor 14:29).

# Why the unsaid does not stay gone

**What a body will not say, it does not stop knowing — it converts into story.** An unspoken reality does not dissolve; people fill the silence with assumption and act on the story rather than the truth. Naming it is how the body returns to what is real, so that the Lord can speak into the real thing.

# The four guards (do not open it without these)

1. **The container and the skill are built first.** The deep naming is the *last* move, never the opening one. The cohort earns it across the weeks of reflective listening and the I-heard frame. Never cold.
2. **Bounded power in the room.** People will not name a thing that indicts those with power over them while those people sit there — the silence *is* the knot, operating. We do not flatten the room; we submit the conversation to the one Head under whom every member sits (1 Cor 12; Eph 1:22–23). Where that submission is not yet real, go to the standing-pair 1:1 route below.
3. **Voluntary.** No one is required to speak or to disclose.
4. **A clean close is possible.** Work opened and not closed dissipates rather than integrates. Do not open what the gathering has no time or capacity to close.

# The movements

**0. Build the container and the skill** — across Going Deeper (reflective listening; the I-heard discipline of Wk 7).

**1. Ground truths.** Each member names what is real *for them*; a partner reflects back what they heard before anyone responds. We are assembling the parts, not winning.

**2. Name it, and locate its level.** Put the knot plainly on the table, then ask whose it is: this cohort? a pair? those who lead? or *how this gathering is being led*? Naming the wrong level mis-fires the work.

**3. Let silence do the heavy lifting.** After it is named, do not rush to fill the silence (Ps 46:10). The Companion holds the container open — the same discipline as Wk 7's silent listen.

**4. Bring it to the Lord, not just into the room.** The gathered cohort brings the named knot before Him (Matt 18:20) and listens for what He is saying about us, weighing what surfaces (1 Cor 14:29; discernment of voices). This is the Wk 8 move: the body hearing about itself. The Spirit, not the technique, does the work.

**5. Commitments, accountability, and a clean close.** Each names what they will do, with whom, by when; the cohort may hold one another to it (the Tell). Then close the container deliberately — name what was received, honor those who spoke into risk, release.

**Fallback — when the room cannot hold it.** If the naming produces only silence, or power makes candor unsafe, drop to the **standing-pair 1:1**: ask each member privately *"why the silence?"* and *"what is the real knot here?"*, then resurface to the cohort **aggregated and de-identified**. This is exactly how a stuck room is recovered.

# How to use this card

- It is the **reference behind Week 8**. Print it for the Companion team; read it alongside the Wk 8 lesson plan.
- It uses the **same I-heard discernment** the cohort built in Wk 7 — now turned on the cohort's own life rather than one member's question.
- **Not for:** a cohort whose container is not yet built; a moment when power in the room makes candor unsafe (use the 1:1 route); a session with no time to close.

# IJH source

The canonical protocol is **"Interrogating Reality: Naming What the Body Already Knows"** in **IJH Vol 5** (the Tool Protocols). It rests on **V2.Exp9 — Community as Amplifier** (the Law and the Danger): the same gathering that can hear truth together can also pressure and distort, which is why the guards and the weighing are not optional.

# Attribution

The practice of courageously naming the undiscussable, the reflect-back exchange, and the phrase *"let silence do the heavy lifting"* originate with **Susan Scott, *Fierce Conversations***, and are credited to her. This card does not reproduce her proprietary models; it is an original, scripturally grounded synthesis that carries the practice into formation.
